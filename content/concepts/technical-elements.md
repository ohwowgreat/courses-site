# Technical Elements

The most concretely examinable content in 9607: the four-part toolkit for reading moving image. Drilled all year, tested directly in **Component 2 Section A**, and doubling as the vocabulary of production.

The syllabus frames it as a means, not an end (Syllabus 2027 p.16):

> "Candidates should study a range of texts so that they can discuss, in response to the question, how the following technical elements create specific representations of individuals/groups/events/places, and help to articulate specific messages and values that have social significance."

**Naming alone earns little.** The mark is in technique → meaning → representation. This is the single most repeated correction in the whole course.

**The 2028 Scheme of Work rebuilds its Section A support around exactly this** (ingested 2026-07-20). It reproduces the syllabus element lists "verbatim from the 2028-2030 Syllabus" inside the teaching rows — the lists below are unchanged — and then supplies a large per-element exercise bank: shot-type identification drills, freeze-frame deconstruction, camera power mind maps ("Who is shot from a high/low angle? Who gets the close-ups?"), re-shoot planning, sound audit tables, dialogue-and-power micro-analysis, foley and contrapuntal-sound workshops, mise-en-scène audits, genre deconstruction, editing timeline breakdowns, paper-based re-edits, flashback construction, plus quick retrieval games (shot bingo, "define diegetic in 10 seconds," correct-the-flawed-analysis). Every exercise routes technique through representation of **class, age, gender, ethnicity** — the syllabus's technique → meaning → representation chain, operationalized. It also names representation theorists for the first time: see [[concepts/representation]] and [[analyses/9607-theory-provenance]].

The 2028 edition frames the banks explicitly as "a menu from centres to choose from at different points in the delivery of the course - some could be used in initial teaching of topics, others can be used for retrieval and revision purposes centring on key assessment points."

## The four elements, per the syllabus (p.16)

### Camera: shots, angle, movement, composition

- **shots** — establishing, master, close-up, mid-shot, long, wide, two-shot, aerial, point-of-view, over-the-shoulder, "and variations of these"
- **angle** — high, low, canted
- **movement** — pan, tilt, track, dolly, crane, steadicam, hand-held, zoom, reverse zoom
- **composition** — framing, rule of thirds, depth of field (deep and shallow focus), focus pulls

Teach as meaning pairs, not as a list: low angle grants power, canted angle signals dissonance, shallow focus isolates, hand-held destabilizes.

### Sound

- diegetic and non-diegetic, synchronous/asynchronous, sound effects, sound motif, sound bridge, dialogue, voiceover, mode of address / direct address, sound mixing, sound perspective
- **soundtrack** — score, incidental music, themes and stings, ambient sound

The Scheme of Work flags sound as the weak spot and therefore the opportunity (SoW p.13): "Sound is often neglected, so it is worth doing some targeted work on this, in particularly helping learners to develop a vocabulary of sound terminology, e.g. harmony, dissonance, discordance, sound bed, rhythm, crescendo, pitch, timbre." *(sic — "in particularly" is the source's own slip.)*

Because it is neglected across the candidature, sound is the cheapest way for a script to stand out.

### Mise-en-scène

- **production design** — location, studio, set design, costume and make-up, properties
- lighting, colour design

⚠ Note what is *not* here: **acting and performance are not listed under mise-en-scène**, and the syllabus states separately that "acting is not a skill that is assessed in media studies" (p.30). Body language and expression are also absent from the syllabus bullet, though the Scheme of Work's own glossary includes "expression, movement, symmetry" in its mise-en-scène definition (SoW p.27) — a mild internal disagreement.

For ESL learners this is the highest-yield element: costume, lighting and colour are visible, nameable, and rich in connotation.

### Editing

"This includes transition of image and sound – continuity and non-continuity systems."

- **cutting** — shot/reverse shot, eyeline match, graphic match, action match, jump cut, crosscutting, parallel editing, cutaway, insert
- **other transitions** — dissolve, fade-in, fade-out, wipe, superimposition, long take, short take, slow motion, ellipsis and expansion of time, post-production, visual effects

⚠ **Long take, short take and slow motion are filed under Editing**, not camera. Candidates and teachers routinely misfile them.

Editing is invisible until you make it visible — which is exactly what the cut-counting exercise in [[concepts/the-viewing-protocol]] is for.

## How it is examined

Component 2 Section A: an unseen extract "from a TV drama and will last for a maximum of five minutes. It will be played four times. The screening time will last a total of 30 minutes, including note-taking" (Syllabus 2027 p.23). Then 45 minutes of writing, 25 marks.

The syllabus's coverage caveat is a relief worth telling students (p.23):

> "It is acknowledged that not every one of the technical areas will feature in equal measure in any given extract and other areas may also be credited where appropriate… We do not expect each aspect to be covered in the same degree of detail, but as appropriate to the genre and extract provided and to the discussion of the extract's representation."

So a script does not need to hit all four elements evenly. It needs to hit what the extract actually does, and tie each observation to representation.

## The analytical paragraph frame

The Scheme of Work's expected output shape, assembled from its synthesis questions (SoW p.14): technique named → terminology → meaning created → link to representation → link to the question.

This frame is the direct rehearsal for A1 and A4 in [[classes/media-studies/assessments/9607-s1-assessments|the S1 register]], and it is what CS4, CS6 and CS8 practise.

## Spec note

The 2027 spec screens the extract from **DVD, four times**. Under the 2028–30 syllabus the exam is digital and the extract is embedded in the platform — see [[classes/media-studies/media-studies]] for the straddle. The technical vocabulary transfers unchanged; the **format practice does not**.
