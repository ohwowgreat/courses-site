# Case Study Method

9607 is a case-study subject. Candidates do not answer exam questions from general knowledge; they answer from institutions and texts known in depth. The method governs Component 2 Section B, both Component 4 sections, and shapes the portfolios.

## The syllabus definition

> "Throughout the course candidates are expected to study a wide range of linked texts, referred to as case studies. These will be used to support their responses in the examinations, and might influence their production work as well. Case studies should feature a majority of contemporary texts, although any text may be used to give historical context. **The term contemporary is used to mean a text which was first published no more than approximately five years before the examination year.**" (Syllabus 2027 p.13)

**The five-year rule is operational, not rhetorical.** Teachers routinely assume "contemporary" means "recent" or "within a decade." It does not. For a June 2027 AS entry, the majority of case-study texts should date from roughly 2022 onward. Historical texts are permitted freely, but as *context*, not as the bulk.

**The 2028 Scheme of Work extends the currency logic to teaching extracts** (ingested 2026-07-20). For Section A TV-drama work it now advises selecting "extracts that are **within 5 years of the teaching of each cohort**; and extracts that are produced / distributed by different organisations and a mix of genres," while noting that "a substantial number of extracts which have been used in previous examinations in both summer and winter sessions and across the different time zones" can be re-sourced for practice. Two distinct five-year clocks now exist: case studies date from the **examination year**; teaching extracts from the **teaching of the cohort**.

## How many

| Requirement | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Media areas studied (AS) | **at least one** | Syllabus pp.9, 17 |
| Case studies referenced in C2 Section B | **at least one** | Syllabus p.23 |
| Case study known for C2 Section B teaching | "at least one case study which is known in depth" | SoW p.14 |
| Media forms per C4 Section A debate answer | one or more | Syllabus p.26 |
| Media forms in the C4 Section B answer | **at least two** | Syllabus p.26 |
| A Level debate topics studied | **at least two of three** | Syllabus pp.9, 18 |

Every one of these is a **floor, not a target**. "At least one media area" is not "choose one" — and the syllabus separately requires studying "a range of media forms and media platforms" across the course (p.13), which pushes against a single-area diet.

## ⚠ "Media area" is not "media form"

Two taxonomies that are easy to conflate:

- **Media areas** (Syllabus p.17) — Film · Music · Print · Radio and podcasts · Video games. These are *industry study areas* for the AS contexts work. At least one required.
- **Media forms** (Syllabus p.13) — time-based audio-visual media (film, TV, radio) · print (newspapers, magazines, advertising) · video games. A three-way categorization of *medium*, with the note "There is overlap between these media forms."

Music is a media *area* but not a media *form*. So **studying one media area does not by itself satisfy Component 4 Section B's "at least two media forms" requirement**. This matters for the G12 planning: the Section B answer must span two forms whatever the Grade 11 case study covered.

## The five media areas (Syllabus p.17)

Each carries its own required accompaniment:

- **Film** — specific studios or production companies: production, distribution, exhibition, consumption; "accompanied by a study of contemporary film distribution practices (digital cinemas, DVD, 3D, downloads and streaming, etc.)"
- **Music** — particular record labels and independent producers; "accompanied by study of wider issues around digital distribution of music"
- **Print** — contemporary newspaper and/or magazine publishing; how print "is adapting to challenges… as a result of technological changes"
- **Radio and podcasts** — production, distribution, audience consumption; "accompanied by a study of the impact of DAB and internet broadcasting… and the development of podcasting as a form"
- **Video games** — a specific game across platforms plus its reception; "accompanied by a study of the impact of next generation capabilities"

The Scheme of Work offers the same set as suggestions and explicitly hedges — "The case study **could include**" (SoW p.14) — so this is not a closed list at the SoW level. The syllabus is the authority on what is permitted.

## Depth: what "known in depth" means

Cambridge never defines it. The from-2021 SoW supplied an operational definition indirectly — the six things a candidate must be able to demonstrate whichever area they chose (SoW pp.14–15):

- the influence and issues of media ownership
- convergence and synergy in production, distribution, marketing and consumption
- the significance of particular technologies in production, distribution, marketing and consumption
- how these technologies have changed and how they might change in future
- local and global audiences and the differences in media consumption
- their own media consumption and production practices

If a case study can answer all six, it is deep enough. That list is the de facto depth spec — though the source never frames it that way.

⚠ **The 2028 SoW drops that bullet list.** Its replacement depth signal is structural: every media area's activity bank runs the same circuit — ownership/funding → production practices → distribution → marketing → audience consumption — and each area's learning objective is "the interrelationship between **production decisions and consumption decisions**." Same substance, reorganized; the six-bullet list above remains the cleanest checklist and is retained here for that reason, but cite it to the from-2021 edition only. The "case study **could include**" hedge survives verbatim in the 2028 edition, so the five-area list is still open at SoW level.

## Choose for the afterlife

The strongest planning rule in the course, and it is Cambridge's own: "**Note also that the work for Component 2 lays the foundations for Component 4 – particularly the Media Ecology element**" (SoW p.17). Media ecology is synoptic and explicitly recycles earlier work: "Examples from other components **can and should**, be drawn upon" (SoW p.26).

So the Grade 11 case study is chosen twice — once for Paper 2 Section B, and once, silently, for Paper 4 Section B eighteen months later. Pick an institution that can grow across two or more media forms, or the synoptic essay starts from scratch.
