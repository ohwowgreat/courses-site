# BNDS AI Policy

School-wide, from the **A-Level Student Guide 2025-26 §VI** (pp.22–24), inside the Academic Honesty Policy. Applies to every course in this vault.

The policy's structure is unusually practical: rather than a blanket permit-or-ban, it defines **six graded levels** and requires each assignment to declare which level applies.

## The six levels (Guide pp.23–24)

| Level | Name | What is allowed |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | Human-Only Work | Student produces everything without AI help. No disclosure required |
| **1** | AI for Ideation | AI only to clarify the task, surface key terms, or map existing knowledge — **not to generate fresh concepts**. Students still do their own brainstorming. Typical: definitions, locating foundational references, checking feasibility, keyword lists for literature searches |
| **2** | AI for Structuring | Level 1 + AI organizes material — outlines, templates, flowcharts. **No full paragraphs, code blocks, calculations or images generated** |
| **3** | AI for Refinement | Levels 1–2 + AI edits or polishes a human draft: grammar, style, citation format, code linting. **Keep a before-and-after or tracked-changes file** showing what was accepted |
| **4** | AI Co-Creation | Levels 1–3 + student and AI share the labor. Substantial parts AI-generated or transformed, then shaped by the student. **Track who did what and cite the model and version** |
| **5** | AI-Generated for Review | Levels 1–4 + the whole piece is AI-produced, submitted only so humans can critique or annotate it. **Label it "AI-generated"**; grading focuses on the student's analysis, not the AI text |

> *"Always check assignment AI Level"* (Guide p.23). Some assignments **ban AI entirely** — the handbook names exams and personal reflections — and others actively encourage it.

## Attribution requirements (Guide p.22)

Whenever AI is used, students must:

1. **Cite the tool** — e.g. "Generated with assistance from [AI Tool Name]"
2. **Explain how it was used** — e.g. "Used for brainstorming but all analysis is my own"
3. **Cite the prompt** that was used

Instructors may additionally require a disclaimer or a reflection on AI use.

## Permitted and prohibited (Guide p.22)

**Permitted:** assisting learning (brainstorming, clarifying concepts, summarizing readings, generating practice questions); enhancing productivity (grammar checks, citation formatting, organizing research); encouraging creativity (AI output as a *starting point* for original work, with attribution); developing digital literacy.

**Prohibited:** submitting AI-generated work as one's own without citation or permission; using AI to replace independent analysis, original thought or personal reflection unless explicitly allowed; spreading unverified AI content — students "must verify AI-generated content for accuracy."

Misuse follows the Academic Honesty Policy: resubmission, grade penalties, or disciplinary action (Guide p.23).

## The Art & Design worked example (Guide p.24)

The handbook's only subject-specific illustration is for Art & Design:

- **Level 1** — AI summarizes Bauhaus colour theory so the student can plan palette experiments
- **Level 2** — AI offers a clean portfolio layout grid (page order, caption placeholders, margins) that the student fills with original work
- **Level 3** — AI polishes the grammar of an artist statement and reorganizes paragraphs for flow
- **Level 4** — Midjourney, Stable Diffusion or similar generates an image the student then collages, annotates and contextualizes, **detailing the AI's role in the process**

## ⚠ Where school policy meets exam-board policy

These are separate regimes and the exam board is the stricter authority on assessed work. Both must be satisfied.

| | Position |
|---|---|
| **BNDS** | Six levels; Level 4 co-creation explicitly permitted, including generated images in Art & Design, with attribution |
| **Cambridge 9479 (2028-30)** | AI-generated images permitted **only** with the candidate's own role demonstrated and justified — see [[classes/a-level-art-design/a-level-art-design]] |
| **Cambridge 9607 (2027)** | *"Cambridge International has a policy on the use of generative AI by candidates in coursework. The inappropriate use of AI should be treated as a form of plagiarism"* (Syllabus 2027 p.30). The 2027 spec does not otherwise address AI; the 2028-30 spec permits it with documented prompts and justification |

**The practical rule for coursework:** BNDS Level 4 may be permissible for classroom tasks, but anything going to Cambridge as assessed coursework is governed by the syllabus for the exam year. Where they differ, the syllabus wins — and the handbook's own attribution requirements (cite the tool, explain the use, cite the prompt) happen to be exactly what the 2028-30 Art & Design spec asks for, so satisfying BNDS properly also satisfies Cambridge.

⚠ This is the **2025-26** edition of the handbook. AI policy is the section most likely to have been revised for 26-27; confirm before relying on the level definitions.
