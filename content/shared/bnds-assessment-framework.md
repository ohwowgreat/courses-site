# BNDS Assessment Framework

The school-wide grading scheme from the **BNDS A-Level Student Guide 2025-26**, applied by every course in this vault. Each course's schedule workbook embeds these rules; this page is the single reference so course pages don't restate them.

**Sourced directly since 2026-07-19.** The handbook is now in `raw/shared/`; everything below is cited to its own pages rather than inferred from the workbooks that quote it. Reconciling the two surfaced one real conflict — see the notice rule.

**Edition:** 2025-26, and confirmed by the human 2026-07-19 as **the current one — no 26-27 edition exists.** Its content remains valid except where it touches syllabus specifics, which change per subject and per exam year. Treat the framework, curving, notice, retake and AI rules as live; check anything syllabus-shaped against the syllabus.

## Grade composition

| Category | Weight | Definition (per handbook) |
|---|---|---|
| **Attainment** | **50%** | Major, summative. Syllabus content only, exam conditions, curved to CIE past-paper data; should produce a reliable Forecast Grade. Project completions and portfolio milestones count. |
| **Course Skills** | **20%** | Minor, formative. Skills/content relevant to the syllabus, flexible format: quizzes, drafts, practice tasks, homework. |
| **Learning Behaviour** | **10%** | Glossary upkeep, blog punctuality, screening/feedback participation, case-study contributions, correcting work, submitting on time. *(Template default — see below.)* |
| **End of Term Exam** | **20%** | Summative, previously covered content, sat in the school exam window (S1 2026-27: Mon 2027-01-11 – Mon 2027-01-18). |

## Notice rule — resolved: one week

**Attainments carry one week's notice**, per the AL Student Guide p.13 (*"at least one week in advance"*). The shared planning workbook's **14 days**, stated four times, was a stricter reading and is **superseded**.

Non-attainment assessments: **72 hours** (both sources agree).

**No existing announcement date is invalid** — every one currently gives 14 days, which exceeds the requirement. What changes is the *freedom* going forward: announcements no longer have to clear a fortnight, which removes several contortions the stricter rule forced. Where a plan says "14 days" as a *constraint*, it can be relaxed; where it says it as a *fact about an existing date*, it is still accurate.

## Standing rules

- **4–6 attainments per semester** (workbook, Assessment Plan C27); none in the first two teaching weeks.
- **Attainment dropping** — one per semester, late in the semester, but only if **the class has ≥6 attainment grades in MB**, the student has missed none, has no plagiarism or cheating record, no unexcused absences and is always on time, and attends all required grade/programme meetings (Guide p.12). ⚠ **All four courses run four attainments, so no student in any of them is eligible.** That is a consequence of course design, not of student conduct, and is worth saying out loud to students rather than letting them assume otherwise.
- **One grade per week** posted on MB — "at least one grade posted per subject each week" (Guide p.12). Learning Behaviour postings fill the weeks with no CS task or attainment.
- **Marking returned within 5 working days** — "Grades will be posted on MB within five working days" (Guide p.12).
- **Homework capped at 30 minutes per subject per day** (Guide p.13); break homework ≈ 3–4 hours per ten-day holiday (workbook convention).
- **Extensions** must be requested **at least 24 hours before the deadline** (Guide p.13).
- **Absence from an assessment** requires contacting the subject teacher **24 hours before the due time**; illness needs a doctor's note. ⚠ *"If no communication is made with the subject teacher in advance you may be given 0, even if this is due to illness"* (Guide p.8).
- **Late-work cutoff:** *"three weeks before the last school day of the semester"* (Guide p.13). ⚠ For S1 that computes to Fri 2027-01-01, which **falls inside the winter break** — the operative date is unresolved, see [[shared/school-academic-calendar]].
- **Assessment content:** only what has been taught and practised — *"You should have seen similar content or formats beforehand"* — though "the context in which the knowledge or skills are applied may be novel" (Guide p.9).
- Every formative (CS) task adjacent to an attainment is **direct practice for it** (vault convention, not school policy).
- Mid-term report grades (S1: due Thu 2026-10-29) must be fed by an attainment marked in time.
- **Seniors get a reduced assessment load** in the week before the 1 Nov and 1 Dec university deadlines (Guide p.13) — relevant to G12 Media Studies and to [[classes/oxbridge/oxbridge|Oxbridge]].

## Curving and grade boundaries

Attainments are **not recorded as raw marks** (Guide pp.11–12):

- Curved against **CIE past-paper data**, "allowing for where students are on their learning trajectory."
- **More generous in G10, stricter in G11** and later in the year. This matters for [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/pre-a-level-art-design|PAL]], the only G10 course here.
- Grades "reflect your learning trajectory, not what you would score on a CIE exam today."
- Past-paper grade thresholds are "the best starting point" for setting boundaries.
- **Moderation is required** where an attainment runs across multiple classes taught by different teachers; the Head of Department checks large between-class differences and may adjust curves or boundaries.
- *"You will always be told how grade boundaries and curves are decided."*

An attainment "should make a reliable **Forecast Grade**" (Guide p.11).

## Grade conversion (Guide p.11)

| CIE scale | A-Level grade | BNDS GPA |
|---|---|---|
| 90–100 | A* | 4.3 |
| 80–89 | A | 4.0 |
| 70–79 | B | 3.5 |
| 60–69 | C | 3.0 |
| 50–59 | D | 2.5 |
| 40–49 | E | 2.0 |
| below 40 | U | 0 |

**Failing is below 50%** (Guide p.15). **At or below 70%** triggers the Academic Concern Steps — the advisor and A-Level Director are informed and support measures follow (Guide p.13).

## Retake policy (Guide §IV, pp.15–16)

One opportunity to retake a failed course, in the holiday after the semester, with 2–3 teacher meetings for support. Work is "just as challenging" and similar in quantity to the original. The grading policy is explained before finals and **cannot be changed once agreed**. A signed academic-honesty declaration is required; plagiarism means immediate failure with no resubmission.

**A passed retake earns the equivalent of a D**, not the grade actually achieved. A failed retake leaves the original grade standing. Under force majeure, >50% completion plus written parent-signed evidence can pass; without evidence, fail.

Graduating seniors get only **two weeks** for a retake, because their Summer Holiday starts early.

## Definitions, verified against the handbook

The four category definitions in the table above came originally from the workbooks. Checked against Guide p.11 on 2026-07-19, they hold — the handbook's own wording:

- **Attainment 50%** — "Tests only on syllabus content · Done under exam conditions · Curved according to CIE past paper data, allowing for where students are on their learning trajectory (ie more generous curves for G10) · It should make a reliable Forecast Grade." Assessment types: past paper questions.
- **Course Skills 20%** — "Testing on skills and content relevant to the syllabus · Format more flexible than attainment." Types: quizzes, presentations, homework.
- **Learning Behaviour 10%** — "Tracking and incentivising important learning behaviours for the class eg communication, collaboration, inquisitivity." Types: participation, asking questions in class, group work, independent inquiry, correcting work, submitting work on time.
- **End of Term Exam 20%** — "Summative, on previously covered syllabus content." Types: past paper questions.

Note that the handbook itself frames Attainment and End of Term around **past paper questions and exam conditions** — which is exactly the strain the studio and elective courses have been recording. The handbook does add that "Departments may decide more precisely what are deemed acceptable assessments for these different categories," which is the clause under which 9479's Final and PAL's proposed portfolio sitting are legitimate.
