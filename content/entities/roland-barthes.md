# Roland Barthes

The most-used theorist in 9607, and one of only two the from-2021 Scheme of Work names for the AS year (the other is [[entities/stuart-hall]]).

**Carried into the 2028 Scheme of Work** (ingested 2026-07-20), slightly strengthened: the C1 film row now names "Mise-en-Scène – the production of visual meaning: **Roland Barthes' 5 Narrative Codes**" directly, where the from-2021 edition reached the codes only via an external link in C2. He remains the only theorist named in the 2028 SoW's Component 1. The C2 narrative-codes pointer and the Hall encoding/decoding pairing are gone from the 2028 edition — see [[entities/stuart-hall]].

## What Cambridge attributes to him

Two distinct contributions, in two places:

**Semiotics — signs, codes and meaning.** Named as the theory hook for C1 research on both briefs: "Relevant theory: semiotics – **Roland Barthes'** ideas about signs, codes and meaning" (SoW p.8 for film, p.9 for magazine).

**Narrative codes and signification.** In the Component 2 textual-analysis material (SoW p.14):

> "This work does not need to be densely theoretical, but there is an opportunity to use some of **Roland Barthes'** narrative codes to provide some explanatory depth… as well as notions of signification – what is **denoted** at a descriptive level, and what is suggested, implied or coded at a **connotative** level"

Note the hedge that opens that sentence — Cambridge positions Barthes as optional explanatory depth, not required content. See [[analyses/9607-theory-provenance]].

## Where he earns his place in the course

- **[[concepts/media-language]]** — denotation and connotation is the ladder the whole of Unit 2 climbs. The denotation-before-connotation discipline is built into CS1 in [[classes/media-studies/lesson-plans/9607-s1-lesson-01-the-four-key-concepts|L01]] and marked in A1.
- **[[concepts/narrative]]** — the enigma code gives narrative analysis explanatory depth without heavy theory, and connects directly to the C1 film-opening research question "how does the film establish an enigma from the outset?" (CWH p.11).
- **C1 research** — signs and codes are the vocabulary of annotating real openings and covers.
