# Fredric Jameson

The most substantively developed theorist in the whole Scheme of Work — the only one Cambridge gives a full analytical framework rather than a name and a link.

**Carried into the 2028 Scheme of Work verbatim** (ingested 2026-07-20): all five symptoms below, including the Brexit / death of Diana / refugees examples, appear unchanged in the 2028 edition's Postmodern media topic. He remains the only fully developed framework in either SoW.

## What Cambridge attributes to him

"Similarly, and more recently, **Fredric Jameson** diagnoses the postmodern condition as characterised by:" (SoW pp.23–24)

| Symptom | Cambridge's wording |
|---|---|
| **Ahistorical mode** | "lacking a sense of the past – history becomes another set of signifiers which can be played with" |
| **Collapse of high/low** | "A lack of distinction between 'high' and 'low' culture" |
| **Surfaces** | "increasingly there is no time for depth – politics becomes soundbites, protests become slogans, the world is a collection of images" |
| **Affectlessness** | "the predominant mode of irony and 'knowingness' leaves little room for real emotion, but short, sharp intense emotional responses are elicited through surface representations of things of which people have no first-hand experience, e.g.: Brexit, the death of Diana, refugees" |
| **Technologies of reproduction and recirculation** | "e.g.: all social media" |

## Why this list is worth teaching directly

It is the most usable analytical apparatus in Component 4. Each symptom converts into an essay paragraph: name the symptom, supply a contemporary text, show the symptom operating, evaluate whether the diagnosis holds.

It is also, unusually, **exhaustive enough to argue against**. The syllabus requires "the arguments for and against understanding some forms of media as postmodern" (Syllabus 2027 p.18), and a five-point checklist is precisely what a skeptical candidate needs in order to show where a text fails to fit.

## Relation to Debord

Cambridge sequences them deliberately: [[entities/guy-debord]] first (1967, spectacle), then "similarly, and more recently, Fredric Jameson." The implied argument is continuity — a diagnosis of image-saturated culture restated for a later moment. Cambridge does not develop the comparison; a strong candidate could.
