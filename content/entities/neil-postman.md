# Neil Postman

Named, with [[entities/marshall-mcluhan]], as the origin of [[concepts/media-ecology]].

⚠ **Dropped from the 2028 Scheme of Work** (ingested 2026-07-20). The renamed synoptic topic, Changing media environments, names no thinker at all. The genealogy sentence below survives only in the from-2021 document.

## What Cambridge attributes to him

> "The notion of media ecology can be traced back to **Neil Postman** and **Marshall McLuhan**, but its contemporary relevance is clearly driven by the saturation of all aspects of contemporary existence with media and media technologies." (SoW p.25)

Complete attribution. **No idea, work, or argument is credited to him** in either Cambridge document held here. No *Amusing Ourselves to Death*, no technopoly, no distinction between the typographic and televisual mind.

## What is left standing

Stripped of the theorists, what Cambridge actually gives for media ecology is a **method** rather than a theory (SoW pp.25–26):

- historical contrast — "What did the media landscape look like before 1990?" against students' own media diaries
- a premise — contemporary life is saturated with media and "mediatised"
- a temporal orientation — "contrasting the present with the past, and analysing the present in order to develop ideas about the future"

That method is genuinely teachable without either founder. The before/after contrast does most of the analytical work, and the exam rewards "informed, imaginative analysis" (SoW p.26) grounded in contemporary evidence, not theorist name-checking.

But the syllabus separately requires candidates to "use key theories of media studies" (Syllabus 2027 p.13), so the theory layer cannot simply be dropped.

## Research candidate — priority downgraded

Same situation as McLuhan: with the 2028 SoW naming no thinker for the synoptic topic, a Postman reading is now a **choice, not a gap-fill**. If the centre does want a founder-figure for the environment framing, Postman remains the more accessible of the two for ESL learners — his arguments are made in plain prose and worked examples rather than aphorism.

Note also that the from-2021 method this page describes ("What did the media landscape look like before 1990?", dystopian thought experiments) was **replaced** in the 2028 SoW by a three-era periodization and four structured focus areas — see [[concepts/media-ecology]]. Grade 12, Semester 4 content. See [[classes/media-studies/unit-plans/9607-course-map|the course map]].
