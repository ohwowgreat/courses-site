# Marshall McLuhan

Named, with [[entities/neil-postman]], as the origin of [[concepts/media-ecology]] — and given nothing else.

⚠ **Dropped from the 2028 Scheme of Work** (ingested 2026-07-20). The renamed synoptic topic, Changing media environments, names no thinker at all; the "traced back to" genealogy sentence below survives only in the from-2021 document. For the G12 exam under the 2028–30 spec, any McLuhan taught is now doubly a centre choice — Cambridge no longer even gestures at him.

## What Cambridge attributes to him

> "The notion of media ecology can be traced back to **Neil Postman** and **Marshall McLuhan**, but its contemporary relevance is clearly driven by the saturation of all aspects of contemporary existence with media and media technologies." (SoW p.25)

That is the complete attribution. **No idea, book, or coinage is credited to him anywhere in either Cambridge document held in this vault.** No "the medium is the message." No global village. No hot and cool media.

## Why this matters for planning

Media ecology is a compulsory 30-mark synoptic paper — half of Component 4 — and Cambridge's teaching support supplies a genealogy without a theory. The Scheme of Work section on media ecology is also the only part of Components 3 and 4 that cites **no external links at all**.

So the theoretical content of the course's biggest single exam question has to be sourced by the centre. That is a genuine provisioning gap, not an oversight to work around quietly.

⚠ Cambridge also never explains **the ecology metaphor itself** — the idea that media are environments we live inside rather than tools we pick up. The syllabus defines media ecology as "the complex environments within which media texts, producers, distributors, technologies and audiences exist" (Syllabus 2027 p.19), which states the environment framing without unpacking why it is called ecology or what follows from the metaphor.

## Research candidate — priority downgraded

Before the 2028 SoW arrived this was the clearest gap the concept layer had surfaced. It is now a **choice, not a gap**: the 2028 SoW's Changing media environments supplies a structured method (three-era periodization, four focus areas) with no theorists, so the centre can either source a McLuhan reading into `raw/media-studies/resources/readings/` or meet the "key theories" requirement with different thinkers entirely.

Until a reading lands, any McLuhan taught in class is unsourced in this vault. See [[analyses/9607-theory-provenance]].
