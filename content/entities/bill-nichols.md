# Bill Nichols

Named once, for **documentary modes**, in the Component 3 documentary brief. **Carried into the 2028 Scheme of Work** (ingested 2026-07-20) in the same role — "It would also be useful to introduce the notion of documentary modes, using the work of Bill Nichols" — though the 2028 edition drops even the external link, so the six modes are now a bare pointer.

## What Cambridge attributes to him

> "It would also be useful to introduce the notion of documentary modes, using the work of **Bill Nichols**" (SoW p.20)

The Scheme of Work links to an external article on "Nichols' 6 modes of documentary" but **does not name or list the modes**. As with every other theorist in this document, the attribution is a pointer, not content.

## When it becomes relevant

Only if the centre offers or a candidate chooses the **documentary package** for [[classes/media-studies/unit-plans/9607-course-map|Component 3]]. The Scheme of Work makes clear that centres may restrict the choice: "you may decide to offer only one of the Set Briefs outlined in the syllabus, or you may decide to give learners free choice" (SoW p.17).

The BNDS working assumption is that the promotion briefs (music, film) fit a five-period week better than documentary or short film, which demand substantially more shooting and editing time — so Nichols may never be needed. Decision is tracked on the course map.

If the documentary brief is offered, the modes are load-bearing: the Scheme of Work's pitch-feedback prompts ask "What mode would be most appropriate" (SoW p.20), so students cannot pitch without them.

## Where Nichols would sit

The documentary research questions Cambridge suggests (SoW p.20) — "What different kinds of documentaries are there? / What is the purpose of a documentary? / What is the audience for documentary?" — are exactly the questions a modes framework answers. The categorization exercise off a published documentary list ("What different categories of documentary are listed here? What stories get told? Whose stories get told?") is a good empirical route into the same material without leading with the theory.
