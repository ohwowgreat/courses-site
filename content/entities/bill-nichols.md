# Bill Nichols

Bill Nichols is the theorist behind the idea of **documentary modes**. His work is referenced in the Component 3 documentary brief as a way to introduce the six modes of documentary.

## When it becomes relevant

Nichols' modes matter only if you take the **documentary package** for [[classes/media-studies/unit-plans/9607-course-map|Component 3]]. The set brief you work on may be restricted to one option or left as a free choice — this will be confirmed in class.

If you do take the documentary brief, the modes are essential: the pitch-feedback stage asks "What mode would be most appropriate," so you need to know the modes before you can pitch.

## How the modes fit

The modes framework answers the core documentary research questions:

- What different kinds of documentaries are there?
- What is the purpose of a documentary?
- What is the audience for documentary?

One way into the same material is to categorise films from a published documentary list: What different categories are listed here? What stories get told? Whose stories get told?
