# Paul Gilroy

Named in the 2028 Scheme of Work as a representation theorist — **surname only, no idea attributed**. New to the Cambridge-suggested roster; the from-2021 Scheme of Work never mentioned him.

## What Cambridge attributes to him

> "candidates need to learn the concept of Representation - best done with reference to theories of representation where there is a variety of student-friendly materials available - such as **Hall, Van Zoonen, hooks, Gilroy, Alvarado**." (2028 SoW, C2 Section A camera row)

The complete attribution. The identification as Paul Gilroy is contextual: the clause concerns representation of "class, age, gender, ethnicity," and Gilroy is the standard A Level media-studies reference for the ethnicity strand alongside Hall, hooks and Alvarado.

## Status in the course

**Not currently taught at BNDS.** The S1 representation block ([[classes/media-studies/unit-plans/9607-s1-unit-4-representation|U4]]) uses Hall, van Zoonen, Mulvey and hooks; Gilroy appears in no BNDS plan. His naming makes him a candidate for the G12 year — the class/age/gender/ethnicity axes the 2028 SoW attaches to Section A analysis recur across both exam components.
