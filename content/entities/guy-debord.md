# Guy Debord

Debord is the anchor for **The Society of the Spectacle** in the [[concepts/postmodern-media|Postmodern Media]] debate.

## The key claim

> "Guy Debord's 1967 work The Society of the Spectacle is a critique of media-saturated capitalist culture is still relevant in diagnosing aspects of the postmodern condition"

In short: Debord wrote a 1967 critique of media-saturated capitalist culture, still useful for diagnosing aspects of the postmodern condition.

## How to use him

A worked exercise:

> **Exercise:** Watch a TV news story. What features does it have of 'The Society of the Spectacle'?

This turns an abstract diagnosis into observable features of a real text — the kind of anchoring in specific texts or phenomena that marks a sophisticated answer.

## A connection worth making

**Détournement** appears in [[concepts/power-and-the-media|Power and the Media]] as a strategy of resistance, without attribution. It comes from the same tradition as Debord: the spectacle is the diagnosis, and détournement is the counter-move. Linking the two gives you a genuine synthesis to draw on.
