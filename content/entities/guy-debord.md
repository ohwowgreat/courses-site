# Guy Debord

Named in the Scheme of Work for **The Society of the Spectacle**, as an anchor for the [[concepts/postmodern-media]] debate.

## What Cambridge attributes to him

> "**Guy Debord's 1967 work The Society of the Spectacle** is a critique of media-saturated capitalist culture is still relevant in diagnosing aspects of the postmodern condition" (SoW p.23)

*(The sentence is ungrammatical in the source — a doubled verb. Quoted as printed.)*

Cambridge supplies a link to an illustrated guide and no further explanation of the concept. The claim in the wiki is therefore limited to: Debord wrote a 1967 critique of media-saturated capitalist culture, which Cambridge considers still useful for diagnosing the postmodern condition.

## Where he is used

The Scheme of Work gives one exercise (SoW p.24):

> "**Exercise:** Watch a TV news story. What features does it have of 'The Society of the Spectacle'?"

That is a good, tight task — it forces students to convert an abstract diagnosis into observable features of a real text, which is exactly the "anchoring the arguments in specific texts or phenomena" that the same page names as the marker of a sophisticated answer.

## Status under the 2028 Scheme of Work

Carried over (ingested 2026-07-20): the attribution sentence above survives verbatim in the 2028 SoW's Postmodern media topic — doubled verb and all. The TV-news spectacle exercise, however, was **dropped** with the rest of the old activity set; the 2028 activity bank (framework comparison, textual feature analysis, structured debate) never uses Debord directly.

## The missed connection

⚠ **détournement** appears two pages later in [[concepts/power-and-the-media]] as a strategy of resistance (SoW p.25) — and is credited to nobody. Cambridge does not connect it back to Debord or the Situationists, despite naming Debord on p.23. **The 2028 SoW repeats the miss**: "strategies of detournement" recurs in its Power topic, still unattributed.

That link is worth making in class: the same tradition supplies both the diagnosis (spectacle) and the counter-move (détournement). It is a genuine synthesis available to a strong candidate, and Cambridge leaves it on the table.
