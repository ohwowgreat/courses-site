## Printmaking

These are the materials for the printmaking part of Unit 4.

**Three-class linocut sequence.** Across three classes you will: (1) get an introduction to relief printing, cover safety, transfer your design using tracing paper, and begin carving; (2) refine your carving, ink with a brayer, make test prints and your first prints on good paper; (3) make final prints, experiment with multi-color and layering, and take part in reflection and a gallery walk. Includes a materials list, formative and summative assessment with its own 100% rubric, and two extensions: a 3–4 print series, or artist research on Dürer, Hiroshige or a contemporary printmaker.

**A5 drawing brief (set as homework before the unit).** Make three A5 drawings that will work as linocuts. Rules: A5 with a 1 cm border; one clear subject readable "in 2 seconds from far away"; no tiny background detail. The key constraint is **"Linocut = no middle values"** — the parts you carve go white, so suggest "grey" with lines, cross-hatching, dots or pattern, never soft pencil shading. Ends with a five-point checklist.

**Carving step by step.** Five illustrated steps: draw your design on paper → transfer it to the lino (carbon paper, or lay the lino on the drawing and rub the back to lift graphite) → cut away the negative space → ink using an inking plate and roller → print with a baren, spoon or hand using circular pressure. Note the **mirror-image rule** — important if your design contains text — and the irreversibility rule: "you can only carve more material away, but you can't put it back."

**Color mixing guide.** An RYB subtractive color-mixing poster: primary/secondary/tertiary star, equal-amount vs proportional mixes with ratios (e.g. 2 red : 1 blue = magenta), tints/tones/shades, and a wavelength spectrum. This supports the multi-color printing session and general color theory.
