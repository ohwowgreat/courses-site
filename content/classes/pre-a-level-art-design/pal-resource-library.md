# PAL Resource Library

Catalog of the teaching material in `raw/pre-a-level-art-design/resources/`. **Catalogued, not transcribed** — these are publisher and museum-credited sources, so this page records what each holds and where it fits, and the files themselves stay the source of truth.

Status column distinguishes **in use** (a plan cites it) from **reference** (kept because it is useful, not part of the sequence). See [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/unit-plans/pal-semester-1-plan|the semester plan]] for what is actually taught when.

## Image sets

**`Visual Elements Sample Work/`** — 17 exemplars for visual-elements analysis, weighted toward photography:

- **Photography:** Walker Evans (*Alabama Cotton Tenant Farmer Wife*, 1936), August Sander (*Artists' Party in Cologne*, c.1930), Carleton Watkins (*Arbutus Menziesii*), Emmet Gowin (*Edith, Chincoteague*, 1967), Frederick H. Evans (*Lincoln Cathedral*, 1898), Eugène Atget (*Versailles* ×2, 1901–02), Gerry Johansson (×2), Albert Elm
- **Painting / works on paper:** Ad Reinhardt (*Number 107*), Adolph Gottlieb (*Blast I*, *Descending Arrow*), Arnulf Rainer (*Untitled*), Fernand Léger (*The Mirror*), Jacob Lawrence (*They were very poor*)

Because the set leans photographic, it **doubles as a U2 Photography reference** — Atget, Watkins and Evans are series-and-structure exemplars, not just visual-elements ones. Complements the image-slide-library rather than duplicating it.

## Printmaking practicals (ingested 2026-07-19)

The making half of U4's printmaking variant. All four are short, concrete and student-facing — unlike the decks, these can go straight into students' hands.

| Item | Covers | Status |
|---|---|---|
| `Printmaking Lesson Plan.pdf` | 6 pages, headed "Printmaking · G10". A complete **three-class linocut sequence**: (1) intro to relief printing, safety, design transfer via tracing paper, begin carving; (2) refine carving, ink with a brayer, test prints, first prints on good paper; (3) final prints, multi-color and layering experiments, reflection and gallery walk. Materials list, formative and summative assessment, its own 100% rubric, and two extensions (a 3–4 print series; artist research on Dürer, Hiroshige or a contemporary printmaker) | **In use — the spine of the printmaking variant.** ⚠ Written for 45-minute singles; PAL runs 90-minute doubles. See the variant page for the remap |
| `A5 Drawing Guide for Linocut.docx` | 1 page, student-facing. Task: **three A5 drawings that will work as linocuts.** Rules: A5 with a 1 cm border, one clear subject readable "in 2 seconds from far away," no tiny background detail. The load-bearing constraint: **"Linocut = no middle values"** — not carved prints, carved goes white — so "grey" must be suggested with lines, cross-hatching, dots or pattern, never soft pencil shading. Closes with a five-point checklist | **In use — the design brief.** Best set as homework ahead of the unit; the variant plan assumes this |
| `Carving Step by Step Instructions.pdf` | 2 pages, five illustrated steps: design on paper → transfer to lino (carbon paper, or lay lino on the drawing and rub the back to lift graphite) → cut away the negative space → ink via an inking plate and roller → print with a baren, spoon or hand using circular pressure. Twice states the **mirror-image rule** — critical if the design contains text — and the irreversibility rule: "you can only carve more material away, but you can't put it back" | **In use — the bench handout.** Pairs with the A5 guide |
| `Color Mixing Guide - 1.jpg` · `- 2.jpg` | **Sheet 1:** an RYB subtractive color-mixing poster — primary/secondary/tertiary star, equal-amount vs proportional mixes with ratios (2 red : 1 blue = magenta, and so on), tints/tones/shades, and a wavelength spectrum. **Sheet 2:** a paint-recipe chart, 22 mixes on a 10-part formula (e.g. bright orange = 8 CY + 2 CR) | **Reference.** Sheet 1 supports the multi-color printing session and doubles as general color theory. ⚠ Sheet 2's pigment abbreviations (CY, CR, TW, PB, AC, SG, PG, YO, BU, VDB) are **never expanded in the source** — unusable by students as-is without a key, and it is written for paint, not printing ink |
