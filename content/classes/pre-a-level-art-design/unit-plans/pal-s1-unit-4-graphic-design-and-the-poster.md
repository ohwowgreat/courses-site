## What this unit does

Spine: **"Communicate · audience."Three** Wednesday doubles (W16, W17, W19 — W18 is lost to the winter break) turn composition into communication: one session analyzing what a poster must do (message, audience, hierarchy) and testing thumbnails and type, then the final poster — handmade or digital — for A4, then documentation. The last session closes the S1 arc with the confirmed End-of-Term portfolio **and the A5 sketchbook submission** (both handed in that day).

## Two routes through the same slot

U4's two labels — Graphic Design and Printmaking — are **interchangeable and occupy the same three Wednesdays**. As of 2026-07-19 both are live:

| | **Poster** (default) | **Printmaking** (contingent) |
|---|---|---|
| Status | **Current.** Everything below describes this route | Drafted, not adopted — [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/lesson-plans/pal-s1-u4-printmaking-variant\|full variant plan]] |
| A4 task | Final poster, handmade or digital | Final linocut print |
| A4 marking focus | Hierarchy, typography, message, audience | Carving control, ink and registration, two-tone legibility |
| CS4 | Thumbnail set + poster analysis | Proof + annotation |
| Teaching material | ⚠ **none in `raw/`** | Well supplied — lesson plan, 37-slide history deck, two handouts, color guides, two videos |
| Materials needed | Paper, pens, or lab computers | Lino, cutters, ink, brayers, barens — ✅ **already held** |

**The trigger is student demand** (confirmed with the human 2026-07-19). The signal already exists: the [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/pal-resource-library|Introduction Survey]] at session 1 asks students to self-rate "Print Making" and name a preferred area of study.

**There is no procurement constraint** — materials for both routes are already on hand. The decision follows the students and can be made late; the only real deadline is notice, since A4 needs one week's warning of what it actually is.

Note the irony worth acting on: the *default* route is the **unresourced** one. If the poster stays, U4 still needs teaching material built from scratch.
