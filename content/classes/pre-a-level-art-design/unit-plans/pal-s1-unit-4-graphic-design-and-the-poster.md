## What this unit does

Spine: **"Communicate · audience."** Across three Wednesday doubles you turn composition into communication: first you analyze what a poster must do (message, audience, hierarchy) and test thumbnails and type; then you make the final poster — handmade or digital — for A4; then you document your work. The last session closes Semester 1 with the End-of-Term portfolio **and the A5 sketchbook submission** (both handed in that day).

## Poster or printmaking

This unit normally runs as the **poster** route, described below. It may instead run as **printmaking**, in which case A4 is a final linocut print (marked on carving control, ink and registration, two-tone legibility) and CS4 is a proof plus annotation. The [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/pal-resource-library|Introduction Survey]] in session 1 asks you to rate "Print Making" and name a preferred area of study. Which route runs will be confirmed in class, at least one week before A4.

## Assessment

- **A4 (Attainment)** — **2026-12-23 (W17)**: final poster + reflection, handmade or digital. Announced 2026-12-16 (W16), the class before. Marking focus: **hierarchy, typography, message, audience**.
- **CS4 (Course Skills)** — **2026-12-16 (W16)**: thumbnail set + poster analysis, in the same double that announces A4. Focus: hierarchy and audience.
- **EoT (End of Term)** — 2027-01-06 (W19): semester portfolio (four finals + process + reflections). Submitted rather than sat, so the exam window does not apply.
- **A5 (Attainment)** — 2027-01-06 (W19): the **sketchbook**, submitted for its terminal attainment mark on the same day as the portfolio. Marking focus: ideas, experimentation, visual research, reflection, presentation.
