# PAL S1 Lesson 07: Light, Shadow and the Series Theme

[[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/pre-a-level-art-design|Pre A Level Art & Design]] · [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/unit-plans/pal-s1-unit-2-photography|Unit 2]] · U2·L2 · ← [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/lesson-plans/pal-s1-lesson-06-framing-and-viewpoint|L06]] | [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/lesson-plans/pal-s1-lesson-08-the-theme-shoot-and-the-contact-sheet-edit|L08]] →

## Goal

Two halves with one join. First: light has *direction and quality*, and moving yourself (or the lamp) is a decision as expressive as framing. Second: A2 is not five nice photos — it is a **series**, one idea looked at repeatedly with intention. The join is the theme: by the end of the double you have one, in writing, approved.

## How it runs

- **Warm-up (10 min)** — homework pairs: one thirds frame and one broken-thirds frame each, your partner guesses which was deliberate. Add vocabulary terms 20–24 (directional / ambient / soft light…) to your sketchbook glossary.
- **Teach: light (15 min)** — *Seeing Photographs* §1 on screen: **front, side, diffused, back** light, one canonical exemplar each; hard vs soft shadows. Then a live demo: one object, one **continuous lamp**, walked around the four positions — what you see is what you get. Exposure control on whatever you shoot with: phones — **tap to focus, AE/AF lock, exposure slider**; cameras — **AE lock and exposure compensation**.

- **Light circuit (20 min)** — in pairs: one subject (a classmate or an object), four frames — window side-light, backlight, diffused, hard lamp. Same subject, four moods; annotate which position made which mood.
- **Teach: the series (15 min)** — what makes photographs *a series*: one subject, idea, or rule; variation with intention; sequence. Exemplars from the shared library's Photographers collection — **Ueda's project folders, Hara's series, Atget's Versailles, Johansson's towns** — plus Cartier-Bresson's decisive moment from §4. Then the **A2 brief**: 6–8 photographs on one theme, selected from many, sequenced, titled, with a short reflection — made over the next two weeks, submitted in class W10.

- **Theme workshop (15 min)** — shortlist three candidate themes (things you can shoot at school or home this fortnight: doors, hands, red, lunch, shadows, pairs…); talk each through with a partner; **write your chosen one in your sketchbook and get it initialed** before leaving.
- **Close (10 min)** — **A2 announced** and posted on MB; homework: ≥15 theme frames before next Wednesday.

## Assessment

**A2** (photographic series + reflection, Wed 2026-11-04) is announced today — 14 days, two classes ahead. Marking focus: **framing, viewpoint, light, series thinking**. **SB1**, the first sketchbook spot-check, is taken during the studio blocks — it checks progress and upkeep (2–3 pages a week since W1), not a full grading.
