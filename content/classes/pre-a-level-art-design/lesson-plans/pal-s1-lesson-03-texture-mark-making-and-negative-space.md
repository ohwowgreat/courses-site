# PAL S1 Lesson 03: Texture, Mark-Making and Negative Space

## Goal

This session covers two ways of looking. First: a surface is drawn by *choosing the right mark*, not by labeling it — so marks and textures are taught as pairs. Second: the shape of the air around an object is as drawable as the object — and often more accurate. Both feed into the final drawing that starts next week, and A1 is announced at the end.

## How it runs

- **Warm-up (10 min)** — mark inventory: copy eight marks from the *Drawing Basics* taxonomy fast (cross-hatch left and right, zigzag, dragged side-of-pencil, spaced dashes, herringbone, serrated rows, wavy horizontals). Name each; they are your vocabulary for the next hour.
- **Studio 1 (25 min)** — *Drawing Exercises 4*, the traced-hand sheet: trace your hand, give each finger a different texture, following the sheet's how-to notes. This forces five distinct mark decisions.
- **Studio 2 (20 min)** — the texture grid: four to six cells, each a real surface from the table (fabric, wood grain, metal, sponge, skin), drawn from observation. Match the mark to the surface; a peer should be able to point from cell to object.
- **Teach (10 min)** — negative space: the drawing subject is the *holes* — the shapes an object cuts out of the air. Figure and ground flip. One demo silhouette.
- **Studio 3 (15 min)** — negative-space study: a chair or stool against a plain wall; draw only the spaces, shade them solid, never draw the chair itself.
- **Close (10 min)** — **A1 and CS1 are announced.** A1 is one final observational drawing plus a short written reflection, made in class in W4–W5, marked on **observation, tone, composition**. CS1 checks next week and covers the four exercise pieces: contour work, the value scale/tonal study, texture, negative space. Both are posted on MB.
