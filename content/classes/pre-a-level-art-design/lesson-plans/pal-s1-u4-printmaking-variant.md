# PAL S1 U4: Printmaking Variant

[[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/pre-a-level-art-design|Pre A Level Art & Design]] · [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/unit-plans/pal-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]] · [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/unit-plans/pal-s1-unit-4-graphic-design-and-the-poster|Unit 4]]

> [!warning] Contingent — not the current default
> **A4 is currently the Graphic Design poster.** This variant runs the same four Wednesdays (W16–19) with linocut in place of the poster, and would only be adopted **based on student demand** (confirmed with the human 2026-07-19).
>
> Both routes are legitimate: U4's two labels are interchangeable and occupy the same slot. This variant tracks the reflowed dates: A4 **Wed 2026-12-23**, **CS4 on Wed 2026-12-16**, and the W19 documentation session on 2027-01-06.
>
> **Everything below is PROPOSED.** It is a remap of a source plan written for a different period length, not a plan the human has approved.

## Why the source plan cannot be run as written

`Printmaking Lesson Plan.pdf` is a good sequence with two structural mismatches:

| | Source plan | PAL reality |
|---|---|---|
| Sessions | 3 classes | **3 Wednesdays** (W16, W17, W19 — W18 lost to the winter break) |
| Period length | 45 min (timings sum to 10+15+15+5) | **90-minute doubles** |
| Assessment | its own 100% rubric, summative "a final set of prints" | **A4** on a fixed date, inside the BNDS 50/20/10/20 framework |
| Design work | in class, "a few ideas" sketched in 15 min | needs to precede the unit — see below |

Doubles help, but the winter break took a session: three single-period classes of content now have to fit **three doubles, only two of which precede the assessment**. The carving overrun this sequence always produces is the binding risk.

## The load-bearing risk

**Carving is slower than the source plan assumes.** Fifteen minutes of first-time carving produces a barely-started block. If carving is not essentially finished by the end of W17, A4 on W18 becomes a scramble and students print half-carved blocks.

Two mitigations, both built into the sequence below:

1. **Design happens before the unit, as homework** — the `A5 Drawing Guide` task (three A5 drawings) is set at W15 (12-09, the A3 collage sitting) so students arrive at W16 with designs ready.
2. **Carving starts in W16, not W17** — the history content is front-loaded into the first half of the W16 double, transfer and carving take the second half.

Carving cannot be sent home: the tools are sharp and the safety briefing is a supervised, in-room item.

## Session sequence (proposed)

⚠ **Reflowed 2026-07-19.** W18 (12-30) no longer exists, so carving and printing are compressed into **two consecutive Wednesdays before the break**, with documentation after it. This is tighter than the poster route and the carving risk is correspondingly higher.

### W16 · Wed 2026-12-16 · S15 — Context, transfer, carve · **CS4**

| Time | Content |
|---|---|
| 0–30 | **Printmaking as an image culture**, `Printmaking.pptx` slides 1–9: Ukiyo-e, the floating world, prints as cheap multiples "designed to travel." Anchor question — *why print instead of paint once?* Slides 13–25 abbreviated: matrix / ink / paper, relief vs intaglio, Dürer to Warhol |
| 30–40 | **Safety briefing** — carve away from body and hands. Non-negotiable, before tools are issued |
| 40–55 | **Transfer**: `Carving Step by Step` steps 1–2, both routes demonstrated. **The mirror rule**, said twice |
| 55–90 | Carving begins, and must go hard. **CS4 logged** at the end: one annotated design sheet showing which areas print and which are cut away |

**Homework set at W15 (12-09):** the three A5 drawings from `A5 Drawing Guide`. Non-negotiable — a student without a design cannot start, and there is no spare session.

 today**, seven days ahead.

### W17 · Wed 2026-12-23 · S16 — **A4: carve, ink, print**

| Time | Content |
|---|---|
| 0–30 | Finish carving. Hard stop at 30 minutes — blocks go to ink as they are |
| 30–45 | Inking demonstration and one test proof each on scrap |
| 45–80 | **A4 sitting.** Ink and pull a final edition on good paper; at least one resolved print submitted. Multiple impressions encouraged, both for quality and as insurance against a spoiled pull |
| 80–90 | Prints laid flat to dry and labeled; blocks and tools cleaned |

⚠ **This session carries both the end of carving and the whole of printing.** It is the single riskiest session in either route. Mitigation: the W16 double must end with blocks substantially carved, and simple bold designs should be actively steered toward at the design stage.

### W19 · Wed 2027-01-06 · S17 — Editioning, reflection, documentation

| Time | Content |
|---|---|
| 0–25 | Further impressions; second colour and layering for those ready — `Color Mixing Guide` sheet 1. Optional, not required to pass |
| 25–45 | **Gallery walk.** All prints displayed; the source plan's reflection prompts |
| 45–90 | **Semester portfolio documentation** and **EoT portfolio due** — unchanged from the poster route |

Three weeks separate the printing from this session, which matters less here than it would for a making assessment: the prints are already made, and this session documents rather than produces.

## Materials — already held

**The materials for both routes are already on hand.** There is no procurement lead time and therefore **no early deadline on the variant decision** — an earlier draft of this page assumed one and recommended deciding by W10. That recommendation is withdrawn.

**The decision follows the students**: it is made when they make it. Practically the only constraint left is notice — A4 needs one week's warning of what it is (see [[shared/bnds-assessment-framework]]), so the route must be settled a session before the announcement, not months ahead.

## Assessment reconciliation

The source plan carries **its own rubric** — Theme Exploration 20% (relevance 10, conceptual depth 10) · Technical Skills 40% (carving 20, inking and printing 20) · Creativity and Design 30% (composition 15, aesthetic appeal 15) · Presentation 10% (final outcome 7, supporting work 3).

⚠ **This is not reconciled with the BNDS framework and should not be adopted as-is.** Two problems:

1. It is a **100% task rubric**, whereas [[shared/bnds-assessment-framework|the BNDS scheme]] splits the grade Attainment 50 / Course Skills 20 / Learning Behaviour 10 / End of Term 20. The rubric can only live *inside* A4 as its marking criteria, not alongside the framework.
2. **"Relevance to Theme" (10%) assumes a theme that PAL S1 does not have.** The four units run Drawing → Photography → Collage → Poster with no unifying theme recorded anywhere. Either that criterion is dropped, or U4 acquires a theme — a decision, not a detail.

Left as a recorded disagreement rather than resolved. If this variant is adopted, the rubric needs a deliberate pass.

## Extensions from the source

- **Linocut series** — 3–4 prints from one block exploring color, layering or added elements. Fits W19 naturally.
- **Artist research** — a short presentation on a printmaker. The source names Dürer and Hiroshige; `Printmaking.pptx` already supplies Dürer, Rembrandt, Daumier, Toulouse-Lautrec and Warhol, and the Ukiyo-e section makes Hiroshige and Hokusai easy to reach.
