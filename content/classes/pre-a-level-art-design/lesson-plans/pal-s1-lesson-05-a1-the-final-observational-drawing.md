## Goal

A working session with a deadline, not an exam-room ritual: the same station, the same drawing, one double to bring it from decided-everywhere to finished — full tonal range, texture where it earns its place, edges resolved. The reflection is part of the submission, not an afterthought: A1 is a drawing *and* the evidence the student can see what they did.

## How it runs

- **Set-up (5 min)** — stations rebuilt from last week's photographs; drawings out; criteria on the board: **observation · tone · composition**. Rule stated once: from here it is your eyes and your paper — no phones, no neighbor's drawing.
- **Studio (60 min)** — finish the drawing. Suggested arc, on the board as a checklist, not a script: push the full tonal range first (darkest darks in, before caution sets in) → texture passes where the surface demands it → edges and cast shadows → step back at arm's length twice, at 20 and 50 minutes. Teacher circulates; prompts only ("where is your darkest dark?"), no hands on drawings today.
- **Reflection (15 min)** — one page in the sketchbook, scaffolded for the ESL room: "I chose this viewpoint because …" / "The light comes from …, so the darkest tones are …" / "The texture I am most happy with is … because …" / "The hardest part was …" / "Next time I will …" Full sentences, at least four of the five frames.
- **Close (10 min)** — drawings and reflections submitted together. **HW1 briefed**: over the break, ≥20 photographs of *one* subject — varied viewpoint, varied light, no editing — due Wed 10-14; it opens Unit 2. Reminder: no class next Wednesday (National Day, 10-07).

## Assessment

**A1 — final observational drawing + reflection**, sat in class today. Marking focus **observation, tone, composition**; marked against the criteria as announced, returned with feedback within five working days, and feeds the mid-term grades due Thu 2026-10-29. W5 is the earliest legal attainment slot (no attainment in the first two weeks). Register: [[classes/pre-a-level-art-design/assessments/pal-s1-assessments|PAL S1 Assessments]].
