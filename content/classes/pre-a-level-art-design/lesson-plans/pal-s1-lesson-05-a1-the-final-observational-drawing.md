## Goal

This is a working session with a deadline. You have one double to bring your drawing from decided-everywhere to finished — full tonal range, texture where it earns its place, edges resolved. The reflection is part of the submission: A1 is a drawing *and* your evidence that you can see what you did.

## How it runs

- **Set-up (5 min)** — stations are rebuilt from last week's photographs; drawings out; criteria on the board: **observation · tone · composition**. From here it is your eyes and your paper — no phones, no neighbor's drawing.
- **Studio (60 min)** — finish the drawing. Suggested arc, as a checklist: push the full tonal range first (darkest darks in, before caution sets in) → texture passes where the surface demands it → edges and cast shadows → step back at arm's length twice, at 20 and 50 minutes. No hands on drawings today.
- **Reflection (15 min)** — one page in the sketchbook: "I chose this viewpoint because …" / "The light comes from …, so the darkest tones are …" / "The texture I am most happy with is … because …" / "The hardest part was …" / "Next time I will …" Use full sentences, at least four of the five frames.
- **Close (10 min)** — submit drawings and reflections together. **HW1 briefed**: over the break, take ≥20 photographs of *one* subject — varied viewpoint, varied light, no editing — due Wed 10-14; it opens Unit 2. There is no class next Wednesday (National Day, 10-07).

## Assessment

**A1 — final observational drawing + reflection**, sat in class today. Marking focus: **observation, tone, composition**; marked against the announced criteria, returned with feedback within five working days, and feeds the mid-term grades due Thu 2026-10-29.
