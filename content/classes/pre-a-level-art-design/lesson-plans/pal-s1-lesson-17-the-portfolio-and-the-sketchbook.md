## Goal

The semester ends by choosing. Four finals, a term of process, forty-plus sketchbook pages — this double is for turning that into **one organized portfolio that argues you can see**, and for handing over the book that shows how you got there. Selection and sequence, one last time, at semester scale.

## How it runs

- **Set-up (5 min)** — everything out: the four finals (drawing, series, collage, poster), process work, sketchbooks. The submission checklist from the portfolio spec on the board.
- **What a portfolio argues (10 min)** — a portfolio is not a folder, it is a claim with evidence: **4–5 of your strongest works**, each with supporting material you choose (intention, reflections, sketches, tests, process photos — no single required formula), in **one clearly organized PDF or slide file**. Choose what represents you, not what took longest.
- **Studio: document and assemble (50 min)** — photograph or scan anything not yet digital (flat, even light, square-on — the image-quality rules from the spec); select; sequence (strongest first and last); one line of intention per work; export as one file, named properly. **Submit before the close.**
- **A5: the sketchbook (15 min)** — settle your final pages, put your name on the spine, and mark the page you're proudest of with a paper slip. Books are collected: the fifth attainment, marked on **ideas, experimentation, visual research, reflection, presentation**.
- **Close (10 min)** — the **End of Semester Survey**: themes you'd want next semester, formats, freedom, what worked, the dream project. Ten quiet minutes. Exam week is next Wednesday (no class); everything returns **Wed 2027-01-20** with grades and 1:1 feedback.

## Assessment

**EoT — the semester portfolio (20%)**, submitted in session and reviewed during the exam window (no sitting). **A5 — the sketchbook**, the whole semester's book, submitted alongside it. Both are marked to the published criteria; grades and comments come at the return session, Wed 2027-01-20.
