# 9479 S2 Unit 1: Foundations on the Investigation

[[classes/a-level-art-design/a-level-art-design|A Level Art & Design]] · [[classes/a-level-art-design/unit-plans/9479-semester-2-plan|Semester 2 Plan]] · Unit 1 of 5 · [[classes/a-level-art-design/unit-plans/9479-s2-unit-2-recording-and-sustained-study|Unit 2]] →

## What this unit does

Opens Component 3 at its foundations stage. The student reads a 90/100 investigation and names what earns the marks, then proposes three candidate themes and defends them one to one; each is screened against primary sources the student can actually reach and against all eight pipeline stages. One theme is **locked in writing** — proposed by the student, approved by the teacher against the exemplar criteria — and the unit closes with a statement of intent in the student's own words, a research plan, and the first primary-source shoot. Locked means locked: the investigation runs on this theme to submission next December.

## Dates & span

Weeks S1–S3 (Mon 2027-02-21 → ~2027-03-13), 15 sessions. Dates are the schedule's provisional spring anchors, not calendar-verified — see the [[classes/a-level-art-design/unit-plans/9479-semester-2-plan|semester plan]].

## Sequence of work

| Code | Session | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| U1·L1 | C3 briefing and the weighted 200 marks | How the investigation is marked; what the written element must do |
| U1·L2 | Reading strong investigations | Name what earns the marks in an exemplar |
| U1·L3 | Theme longlist from the winter homework | Three candidates, with references |
| U1·L4 | Consultation round 1, one to one | Defend each candidate to the teacher |
| U1·L5 | Feasibility screens | Test candidates against reachable sources |
| U1·L6 | Proposal drafting: theme and aims | Why this theme can sustain sixteen pages |
| U1·L7 | Primary source plan | Shoots and sittings the student controls |
| U1·L8 | Artist shortlist | Artists who solve the problem, not favourites |
| U1·L9 | Consultation round 2, one to one | A decision or a fix |
| U1·L10 | Feasibility test against the pipeline | Walk all eight stages; fix what breaks. |
| U1·L11 | Theme locked in writing | Signed with the teacher; no restarts after |
| U1·L12 | Statement of intent finalised | The investigation in the student's own words |
| U1·L13 | Research plan | Reading, sources and dates |
| U1·L14 | First primary source shoot | Reference the student will draw from for months |
| U1·L15 | Shoot review and annotation | Annotate what is seen, not what is felt |

## Key content

- **The primary-source plan contains zero found imagery** — "nothing found, nothing borrowed" is the C3 calibration standard, as in S1.
- The artist shortlist is chosen for problems solved, not personal taste.
- The statement of intent is in the student's own words and names what will be investigated and why.
