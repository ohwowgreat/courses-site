# 9479 S1 Unit 4: Development

[[classes/a-level-art-design/a-level-art-design|A Level Art & Design]] · [[classes/a-level-art-design/unit-plans/9479-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]] · Unit 4 of 5 · ← [[classes/a-level-art-design/unit-plans/9479-s1-unit-3-exploration-and-artist-study|Unit 3]] | [[classes/a-level-art-design/unit-plans/9479-s1-unit-5-final-outcome-and-portfolio|Unit 5]] →

## What this unit does

The development stage, under AO3 Develop — "where top-band AO3 is won or lost." Two full compositions are built (the chosen direction, then the genuinely rejected alternative), both are critiqued against the intention, targeted tests answer only what the critique left unresolved, and the unit closes by planning the final outcome completely before A3 reviews the whole development trail. AO3 closes into AO4 in the final lesson.

## Dates & span

Wed 2026-11-11 → Fri 2026-12-11 (W11 → W15), around the second G11 Juniors Days block (Wed–Fri of W14).

## Lessons

- [[classes/a-level-art-design/lesson-plans/9479-s1-lesson-10-composition-1|Lesson 10: Composition 1]] — composed from the student's own studies via tracing-paper overlays, built in a two-day run, resolved and honestly photographed.
- [[classes/a-level-art-design/lesson-plans/9479-s1-lesson-11-composition-2-and-critique|Lesson 11: Composition 2 and Critique]] — the rejected alternative built deliberately faster; full-protocol structured critique; something changes because of what was heard.
- [[classes/a-level-art-design/lesson-plans/9479-s1-lesson-12-targeted-tests-and-direction|Lesson 12: Targeted Tests and Direction]] — "no decoration"; final direction with the rejection in full sentences; written evaluation; Stage 6 page as a decision trail.
- [[classes/a-level-art-design/lesson-plans/9479-s1-lesson-13-final-plan-and-a3|Lesson 13: Final Plan and A3]] — final composition plan, solving thumbnails, palette argued from meaning; A3 sat; the plan amended from feedback.

## Assessment

Gates on **A3, Thu 2026-12-10** — development milestone (compositions 1 and 2, targeted tests, written evaluation, final composition plan) marked against AO3. CS tasks: **CS8** Fri 2026-11-20 (both compositions photographed and laid out), **CS9** Fri 2026-11-27 (targeted tests + critique response) — direct practice for A3. See the [[classes/a-level-art-design/assessments/9479-s1-assessments|full register]].

## Key content

- The one-sentence direction from A2 is physically present at every desk; development that drifts from it is conferenced the same day.
- Two-day builds never restart: "the discipline of carrying a piece is the content." Composition 2's one-day build is intentional pressure — the process moves faster the second time.
- The rejection paragraph (final direction, Fri of Lesson 12) is "the single highest-leverage piece of writing in the AO4 story" — the exemplar's draft comparison page earned full marks on exactly this habit.
- The final plan's bar: a capable stranger could begin the piece from it. Palette and materials are chosen for meaning and named as such.
- Documentation is taught here because it pays at submission: "a bad photograph loses real marks."
