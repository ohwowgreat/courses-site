## Goal

"No decoration." The tests exist only to answer what the critique left unresolved, the direction is chosen and the rejection is written down, and the development trail becomes readable as a sequence of decisions on the Stage 6 page. This is where top-band AO3 is won or lost.

## How it runs

- **Wed 11-25 → Thu 11-26** — two-day tests run: list the unresolved questions from critique, one test planned per question; day 2 opens with (criteria read, date on the board: Thu 2026-12-10), then tests completed, each labeled with the question it answered.
- **Fri 11-27** — tests and both compositions laid out; choose the final direction; write the rejection in full sentences; **CS9 collected** (tests plus critique response).
- **Mon 11-30** — write the evaluation: what the development achieved against the intention, where it fell short, what the final piece must therefore do. Frame provided, then struck; peer read for honesty.
- **Tue 12-01** — the Stage 6 page as a decision trail: studies, compositions, critique, tests, choice; photograph and file; gap brief for the Juniors Days (Wed–Fri).

## Assessment

**CS9** (targeted tests + critique response, written annotation) collects Fri 2026-11-27 — direct practice for A3 Thu 2026-11-26** — see the [[classes/a-level-art-design/assessments/9479-s1-assessments|full register]]. Every test answers a named unresolved question: no test exists because it was enjoyable.
