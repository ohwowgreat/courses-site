## Goal

Component 2 opens on the Cambridge paper itself. **This is the exam.** By Friday you will have read the paper closely and named each question for what it permits and quietly forbids, matched your choice to **proven strengths from C1 and C3** — shown by your portfolio and drawing regimen, not by hope — and locked one question **in writing with a rationale**. The whole semester runs on it, so make the decision against evidence.

## How it runs

- **Mon 02-21 — course reopening and the ESA briefing.** You read the Cambridge paper together: what the ESA is, how it is marked, and the shape of the spring.
- **Tue 02-22 — question analysis.** What each question permits, and what it quietly forbids — the constraints that decide feasibility.
- **Wed 02-23 — strengths audit.** Match questions to proven strengths from C1 and C3, not to preference — the exam rewards what you can already do at pace.
- **Thu 02-24 — question shortlist, one to one.** Defend two candidate questions against the evidence.
- **Fri 02-25 — question locked with rationale; CS1.** You lock your choice in writing. CS1 is that choice with its rationale. **A1** is announced today (exactly 14 days out).

## Key content: choose on evidence, then commit

- ◆ The strengths audit runs on **what your portfolio shows, not on preference** — the exam rewards what you can already do at pace.
- Your question is locked in writing by Friday with a rationale grounded in evidence; there is no restart after the lock.
- The winter regimen closes here — past-paper review notes and drawing-regimen evidence feed the audit directly.
- Recording at exam pace begins in [[classes/a-level-art-design/lesson-plans/9479-s4-lesson-02-recording-at-exam-pace|L02]].
