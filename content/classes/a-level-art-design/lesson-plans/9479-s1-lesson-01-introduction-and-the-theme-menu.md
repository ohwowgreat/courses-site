## Goal

By Friday every student knows how the portfolio is marked, has read what a top-band portfolio actually does, has committed to one theme from the menu, and has begun looking at it. The theme decision is the most consequential of the semester and it is made against criteria, not by mood.

## How it runs

- **Tue 09-01 — opener, no content.** First Session Opener: introductions, getting to know the group, why this subject, how the course runs. Close with the semester map (five units, four attainments, the zero-spare-days truth) and the four AOs in plain words — orientation only, no portfolio work and nothing graded. This cohort runs to a linear June 2028 entry; say out loud that it is a two-year relationship.
- **Wed 09-02** — the portfolio day (absorbs what Tue used to carry): one finished 96/100 portfolio shown end to end, top band vs middle band two pages side by side, then portfolio reading in pairs against the AO-language worksheet. Theme menu presented with criteria, no choosing yet; exit shortlist of two themes with one line of reasoning each.
- **Thu 09-03** — diagnostic drawing (one observed object, pencil only; kept, not marked); **theme commitment** signed off one-to-one against the five criteria; commitment card: theme, three source locations, first shoot date.
- **Fri 09-04** — source folder setup (one phone folder, one physical); line-study demo (continuous then structural); line studies from first-hand objects within the theme.

## Key content: the theme menu (proposed)

A theme earns its place only if it passes all five criteria: (1) primary sources photographable within daily reach all semester; (2) survives four months of looking; (3) genuinely carries line, tone, texture and color; (4) opens both 2D and 3D development; (5) has a deep artist reference pool the teacher can prescribe from.

- **Reflections and Surfaces** — glass, water, screens, mirrors, polished metal in daily reach; distortion gives instant composition.
- **Worn and Weathered** — rust, peeling paint, cracked walls, mended objects; the strongest texture theme, excellent 3D loop into casting and relief.
- **Hands at Work** — people making and doing; figure drawing with a reason. Requires willing subjects: check access before sign-off.
- **Structures of the Everyday** — the stacked, shelved and scaffolded; line and shape powerhouse. The risk is coldness, so annotation must carry the why.
