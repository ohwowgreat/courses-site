**Concepts:** [[concepts/media-language|Media Language]] · [[concepts/technical-elements|Technical Elements]] · [[concepts/media-vocabulary|Media Vocabulary]] · [[concepts/narrative|Narrative]]

## What this unit does

The micro end of media language — semiotics, sign types, the four code families, genre, camera, narrative — built into vocabulary students own under pressure. In parallel, the C1 production strand launches: media area committed in week 2, planning locked to a shootable standard before the National Day break, first footage shot during it. Everything feeds A1.

*Numbering note: "U2" here is the S1 calendar band, not the coursebook's "Units 1–8". The coursebook's Unit 2 is also Media Language, but this band covers the micro end; the macro/textual-analysis end sits in band U3.*

## Dates & span

**W2–W6: Mon 2026-09-07 → Fri 10-09** — 15 teaching days. Fri 09-25 lost to Mid-Autumn Festival; the National Day break sits between Wed 09-30 and Fri 10-09; W6 contributes a single session (Fri 10-09), absorbed into L05.

## Lessons

- [[classes/media-studies/lesson-plans/9607-s1-lesson-02-semiotics-how-images-mean|L02 Semiotics — How Images Mean]] — Saussure, denotation/connotation, modes of address, anchorage; **media area committed Wed 09-09**.
- [[classes/media-studies/lesson-plans/9607-s1-lesson-03-sign-types-and-codes|L03 Sign Types and Codes]] — Peirce's triad, the four code families, cold-reading unseen stills; vocabulary made a matter of record.
- [[classes/media-studies/lesson-plans/9607-s1-lesson-04-genre-and-camera|L04 Genre and Camera]] — Altman/Neale's industry–audience contract plus camera vocabulary at speed, proven on unlabeled clips.
- [[classes/media-studies/lesson-plans/9607-s1-lesson-05-narrative-and-the-planning-lock|L05 Narrative and the Planning Lock]] — Todorov/Propp; C1 planning peer-reviewed and locked; HW1 and before the break.

## Key content

- Theorists: Saussure (signifier/signified), Peirce (icon/index/symbol), Altman and Neale (genre as agreement), Todorov (equilibrium), Propp (character functions).
- The four code families — technical, symbolic, written, audio — as the two-year analytical checklist.
- C1 milestones: media area committed (feeds the L12 case-study document); planning suite locked against the shootability checklist Wed 09-30; break production window (shoots, 3–4 h); sound and editing vocabulary debut on their own footage.
- ESL thread: EN/ZH connotation-by-culture examples built in deliberately; lexicon grows toward CS3.
