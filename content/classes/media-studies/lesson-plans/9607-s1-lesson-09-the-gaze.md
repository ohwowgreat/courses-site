## Goal

Gaze theory taught as live questions, not settled answers. The two-day block builds van Zoonen and Mulvey; the double stress-tests Mulvey against what students actually watch across decades; and hooks reframes the whole week by asking who the text never expected to be looking. CS6 closes the arc: one close-reading paragraph with a theorist working inside it — the exact move A4 and the exam reward.

## How it runs

- **D1–2 (Mon+Tue block):** van Zoonen — gender as performance, worked on two ads; day 2 retrieval, then Mulvey stated precisely: the look of the camera, the look of characters, the look of the audience.
- **D3 (Wed, double):** paired clips across decades screened and compared — does the gaze hold, shift, or invert; groups argue a verdict with timecoded evidence; review harvests the best evidence lines.
- **D4 (Fri, single):** bell hooks — the oppositional gaze as refusal and rereading; **CS6 written** (one close-reading paragraph, theorist embedded) today: portfolio due Fri 11-27, exactly 14 days.
