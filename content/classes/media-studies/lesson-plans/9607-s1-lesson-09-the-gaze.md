## What you will learn

Gaze theory as open questions rather than fixed answers. Over the two-day block you build van Zoonen and Mulvey; the Wednesday double tests Mulvey against what audiences actually watch across decades; and bell hooks asks who a text never expected to be watching. CS6 closes the week: one close-reading paragraph with a theorist working inside it — the same move A4 and the exam reward.

## How it runs

- **D1–2 (Mon+Tue block):** van Zoonen — gender as performance, worked on two ads; day 2 retrieval, then Mulvey stated precisely: the look of the camera, the look of characters, the look of the audience.
- **D3 (Wed, double):** paired clips across decades screened and compared — does the gaze hold, shift, or invert? Groups argue a verdict with timecoded evidence, and the review collects the best evidence lines.
- **D4 (Fri, single):** bell hooks — the oppositional gaze as refusal and rereading; **you write CS6** today (one close-reading paragraph, theorist embedded). Portfolio is due Fri 11-27, exactly 14 days later.
