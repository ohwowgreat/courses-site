## Goal

The sign is taken apart and reassembled. Monday and Tuesday run as one idea: what a sign shows versus what it suggests, held apart on Saussure's terms. Wednesday turns the lens on address — who the text thinks you are — and closes with a commitment that matters downstream: the media area each student will still be feeding in May. Friday's anchorage work is where the CS2 annotations come due and get tested.

## How it runs

- **D1–2 (Mon+Tue block):** Saussure input (signifier, signified, sign); station work separating "shows" from "suggests" on eight images; day 2 opens with five-minute retrieval, then connotation chains built and challenged in pairs, with cultural variation in connotation surfaced deliberately through EN/ZH examples.
- **D3 (Wed, double):** modes of address across three contrasting texts; students profile who each text thinks it is talking to; final 15 minutes — **media area committed**, logged on the blog with one line of justification each.
- **D4 (Fri, single):** anchorage and relay through caption-swap exercises (same image, three captions, meaning moves); CS2 annotations submitted and peer-checked against the exemplar before teacher collection.
