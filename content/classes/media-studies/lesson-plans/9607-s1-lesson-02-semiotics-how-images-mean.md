## Goal

You take a sign apart and put it back together. Monday and Tuesday run as one idea: what a sign shows versus what it suggests, using Saussure's terms. Wednesday looks at address — who the text thinks you are — and closes with a decision that matters later: the media area you will keep working with through to May. On Friday your CS2 annotations are due and get tested through anchorage work.

## How it runs

- **D1–2 (Mon+Tue block):** Saussure input (signifier, signified, sign); station work separating "shows" from "suggests" on eight images. Day 2 opens with a five-minute retrieval, then you build and challenge connotation chains in pairs, using EN/ZH examples to show how connotation varies across cultures.
- **D3 (Wed, double):** modes of address across three contrasting texts; you profile who each text thinks it is talking to. In the final 15 minutes you commit to your media area and log it on the blog with one line of justification.
- **D4 (Fri, single):** anchorage and relay through caption-swap exercises (same image, three captions, meaning moves); you submit your CS2 annotations and peer-check them against the exemplar before the teacher collects them.
