## Goal

This week Component 2 becomes the destination. Morley's work explains why the same text reads differently to different audiences; ownership explains why texts say what they say; and the Wednesday double takes you through Section A by writing one with the mark scheme open on the desk. On Friday you open the case-study file that A4, the End of Term Exam and Paper 2 will all draw from.

## How it runs

- **D1 (Mon, single):** Morley and the Nationwide study, compressed to the usable claim; you replicate it in miniature — one text, readings gathered, grouped, explained.
- **D2 (Tue, single):** ownership — PSB versus commercial, funding follows content; you trace one visible decision in a text to who pays for it.
- **D3 (Wed, double — CS8):** Section A written with the mark scheme visible, part collaborative by design; band language annotated in the margins as you write; review maps your paragraphs to bands.
- **D4 (Fri, single):** You open the C2 case-study document from the template, build its structure, and make and date your first entries. This day may fall on Foreign Culture Day; whether the session runs as normal will be confirmed in class.
