## Goal

This last teaching week removes surprises from the exam's format, timing, and mark scheme. HW2 is due Monday and feeds into the week. The Wednesday double is a full Section B under exam time, peer-marked so you read the mark scheme as a marker once before being marked by it. Friday names exactly where marks are lost.

## How it runs

### D1 — Mon 01-04: the paper, end to end

- **HW2 in (10 min)** — break packs collected; a thirty-second stand-up per student: which cell did your essay plan target?
- **The walk-through (25 min)** — the full Paper 2 walked end to end, using the *Your Honor* rehearsal paper, following the rubric's own clock: **30 minutes viewing and notes (four screenings, no notes on the first, notes crossed out) · 45 minutes Section A · 45 minutes Section B**. The **frozen Q1 stem is read aloud** — it is identical in every sitting, so know it cold by Friday.
- **Close (10 min)** — write your personal minute budget inside the sections: paragraphs, plan time, check time. You use this budget on Wednesday.

### D2 — Tue 01-05: plans against the clock

- **The drill (30 min)** — **five-minute Section B plans, three rounds**, questions from the CS9 bank: read, plan (claim / evidence / counter / response + case-study facts), pens down at five minutes exactly.
- **The compare (10 min)** — swap your round-three plans: could you write this stranger's essay from their plan? If not, what's missing is what the examiner would miss too.
- **Close (5 min)** — WS 4.5 pairing sentences at speed: technique + content in one sentence, five times.

### D3 — Wed 01-06 (double): CS9, full dress

- **Set-up (5 min)** — one question chosen on the day from the bank; exam conditions on.
- **CS9 (45 min)** — the full Section B, timed exactly, on your own case studies.
- **Peer-mark (30 min)** — swap scripts; mark a peer's script **against the official five-criteria rubric**, band words and all, comments in the WS 4.6 comment-bank style. Reading the scheme as a marker is the point; the mark is a by-product.
- **Moderate (10 min)** — the teacher moderates a live sample of three scripts, thinking aloud where the peer marks drifted.

### D4 — Fri 01-08: the AO economy — last teaching day

- **The economics (20 min)** — where the 25 marks sit: **AO1 15 (concepts / contexts / terminology) · AO2 10 (analysis / examples)**, five criteria, best-fit per criterion. Mapped against the class's CS9 results: which criteria the room feeds naturally, which it starves.
- **Two loss points (15 min)** — leave with **two named loss points and the counter-move for each**, written on one card for your revision deck (e.g. "I list techniques without effects → every technique gets a 'so that' clause").
- **Close (10 min)** — exam logistics: the window opens Monday, your slot comes from the exam office, screening facilities are booked, bring nothing but pens. End on the success check: state the full paper structure and per-section minute budget **from memory**.
