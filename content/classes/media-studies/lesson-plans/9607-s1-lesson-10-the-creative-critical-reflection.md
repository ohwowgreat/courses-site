## Goal

One week, one artifact: the creative critical reflection (CCR). It carries a fifth of the A3 marks, because it is where AO2 lives in the portfolio. The rule for all four days is also the success criterion: **cut every sentence that describes instead of reflects.** A description says what you did; a reflection names a decision, a reason, or a change. Your production diaries from L05 are the evidence base this week.

## How it runs

### D1 — Mon 11-16: Q1, against the evidence

- **The artifact (10 min)** — the four CCR questions in student language, EN/ZH; the format menu from the coursebook (director's commentary, voiceover presentation, podcast, screencast). Choose your format by Friday. "Presented creatively, using technology" is a requirement, not a suggestion.
- **Q1 drafted (25 min)** — *conventions and social groups*: how your product uses or challenges conventions, and how it represents — drafted against the blog's own evidence and last week's stereotype audit. The L08 audit findings are Q1's best material: a fixed stereotype is a reflection sentence waiting to happen.
- **Close (10 min)** — add **one honest sentence** that flatters nobody ("we chose the location because it was available, and it shows in the lighting"). Examiners read honesty as reflection.

### D2 — Tue 11-17: Q2, and the diaries pay out

- **Q2 drafted (25 min)** — *audiences and distribution*: who the product engages and how it would really circulate. Use the audience profile from post 3 and the screening response cards as evidence; "young people aged 15–25" is banned as a sentence.
- **Diary harvest (15 min)** — open your **WS 6.4 production diaries** beside your drafts: every entry in the decisions/consequences columns is a ready-made reflection sentence — *decided X because Y; consequence Z*. Transcribe the three best into your drafts.
- **Close (5 min)** — add the second honest sentence.

### D3 — Wed 11-18 (double): Q3, Q4, and CS7

- **Q3 + Q4 drafted (40 min)** — *skills development* (from day-one footage to the rough cut — name what changed and what changed it) and *technology integration* (software, hardware, online — as decisions, not inventory: "we used X *so that* Y").
- **CS7 (40 min)** — full drafts reviewed against the real C1 criteria, by teacher and peers, describe → question → suggest; every draft leaves with a written action list in C1 criteria language.
- **Close (10 min)** — one sample sentence converted live from describe to reflect.

### D4 — Fri 11-20: sentence surgery

This session may fall on Foreign Culture Day; whether it runs will be confirmed in class.

- **The sort (15 min)** — the surgery sheet: ten sample sentences, five descriptive, five reflective; sort them, then name the tell — reflective sentences contain a decision, a reason, or a change; descriptive ones contain a sequence of events.
- **The surgery (25 min)** — run the test on your own draft, sentence by sentence: cut or convert describers. Finalize your format.
- **Post before leaving (5 min)** — post your complete redraft to the blog, dated. A3 is one week out; the CCR is now portfolio material, not homework.
