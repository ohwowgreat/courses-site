## What you will learn

Genre theory and camera vocabulary come together in this lesson because A1 asks you to use them in the same paragraph. You study Altman and Neale, who treat genre not as a box of tropes but as an agreement between industry and audience — the version that earns marks. For camera work, you learn verbs before adjectives: what the camera does, then what it does to the viewer. In the Wednesday double you prove the whole lesson in one CS4 paragraph.

## Session by session

- **D1 (Mon, single):** Altman and Neale reduced to the exam-usable core — repetition and difference; you map one genre you know onto the industry–audience contract; exit question: why does the industry need genre more than we do.
- **D2 (Tue, single):** camera vocabulary (shots, angles, movement); paired rapid-fire naming drills on a shot montage; a second pass adds effect — name the move, then name what it does to you; exit ticket: three shots, three effects.
- **D3 (Wed, double):** CS4 — you watch an unlabeled clip menu, attribute genre from codes alone, and write an analytical paragraph in exam register; the final 20 minutes are peer-checked against the A1 criteria this task rehearses.
