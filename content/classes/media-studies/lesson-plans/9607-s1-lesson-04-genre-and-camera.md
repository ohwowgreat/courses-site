## Goal

Genre theory and camera vocabulary land in the same lesson because A1 will demand them in the same paragraph. Altman and Neale reframe genre from a box of tropes into an agreement between industry and audience — the version that earns marks. Camera work is drilled as verbs before adjectives: what the camera does, then what it does to the viewer. The Wednesday double strips the labels off and makes students prove the whole lesson in one CS4 paragraph.

## How it runs

- **D1 (Mon, single):** Altman and Neale compressed to the exam-usable core — repetition and difference; students map one genre they know onto the industry–audience contract; exit: why does the industry need genre more than we do.
- **D2 (Tue, single):** camera vocabulary (shots, angles, movement); paired rapid-fire naming drills on a shot montage; second pass adds effect — name the move, then name what it does to you; exit ticket: three shots, three effects.
- **D3 (Wed, double):** CS4 — unlabeled clip menu screened, genre attributed from codes alone, analytical paragraph written in exam register; final 20 minutes peer-checked against the A1 criteria this task rehearses.
