# Media Studies

> **Start here:** [[classes/media-studies/unit-plans/9607-course-map|Course Map]] (all four semesters) · [[classes/media-studies/unit-plans/9607-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]] · [[classes/media-studies/assessments/9607-s1-assessments|S1 Assessments]] · unit docs in `unit-plans/` · lesson docs in `lesson-plans/` · [[classes/media-studies/course-calendar|Calendar]]

**Cambridge International AS & A Level Media Studies (9607)** at BNDS, cohort 2026–2028: G11 = AS year (2026-27), G12 = A2 year (2027-28). 5 × 45-min lessons/week, ESL learners (EN/ZH scaffolding throughout, strong China-context strand: WeChat/Bilibili/Douyin/iQIYI, NRTA vs BBC, *Dying to Survive*, *Ne Zha*). Graded per [[shared/bnds-assessment-framework]].

## Components

| | C1 Foundation Portfolio | C2 Media Texts & Contexts | C3 Advanced Portfolio | C4 Critical Perspectives |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Marks | 50 | 50 | 50 | 60 |
| Weight | 50% AS / 25% AL | 50% AS / 25% AL | 25% AL | 25% AL |
| Nature | Coursework: media product + process evidence + reflection | 2h exam: A = unseen TV-drama extract analysis (25); B = contexts, one of two, case-study-based (25) | Coursework: promotional **campaign** across video + print + web + ~2000-word evaluation (2028-30) | 2h exam: A = media debates, two of three × 15; B = Changing media environments, 30, ≥2 media forms |

C3 briefs (2028-30): music promotion / film promotion / documentary / short film — each = one major video task (group ≤4 allowed) + two individual minor tasks incl. a website home page that must stay live and unchanged through moderation. Case studies must be majority contemporary (≤ ~5 years before the exam year). AI-generated images only with the candidate's role demonstrated and justified.

⚠ **The theory finding (revised 2026-07-20):** neither syllabus names a theorist — all names live in the Schemes of Work's *suggested* columns. The from-2021 SoW named eight thinkers; the **2028 SoW names ten**: it keeps [[entities/roland-barthes|Barthes]], [[entities/stuart-hall|Hall]], [[entities/guy-debord|Debord]], [[entities/fredric-jameson|Jameson]], [[entities/slavoj-zizek|Žižek]] and [[entities/bill-nichols|Nichols]], **adds [[entities/liesbet-van-zoonen|Van Zoonen]], [[entities/bell-hooks|hooks]], [[entities/paul-gilroy|Gilroy]] and [[entities/manuel-alvarado|Alvarado]]** as representation theorists (surnames only), recasts Hall from encoding/decoding to representation, and **drops [[entities/marshall-mcluhan|McLuhan]] and [[entities/neil-postman|Postman]]**. Propp, Todorov and Mulvey remain BNDS additions. Theory is required; specific theorists are not — see [[analyses/9607-theory-provenance]].
