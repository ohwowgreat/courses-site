# Art Appreciation S1 Unit 1: Origins and Ancestral Memory

[[classes/art-appreciation/art-appreciation|Art Appreciation]] · [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]] · Unit 1 of 5 · next: [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-s1-unit-2-narrative-myth-and-allegory|Unit 2 →]]

## What this unit does

Installs the course's method in one move: Warburg's atlas — thematic, non-linear, resemblance over date — said out loud on day one and never apologised for. The constellation logic runs from the earliest images (Chauvet, Lascaux) through Neolithic settlement and ritual objects ("what an object does, not what it depicts") to the first board, which is the course in miniature: the same move A4 and the End-of-Term retrospective repeat at greater scale. The darkroom session on day three makes an image stop being a screen and become a thing light did to a surface — deliberately load-bearing for Unit 4 in December. Key theorist: Aby Warburg. Key material: cave imagery, Neolithic figures, ritual objects.
