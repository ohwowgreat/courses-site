# Unit 1: Origins and Ancestral Memory

[[classes/art-appreciation/art-appreciation|Art Appreciation]] · [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]] · Unit 1 of 5 · next: [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-s1-unit-2-narrative-myth-and-allegory|Unit 2 →]]

## What this unit does

This unit sets up the course's method: Warburg's atlas — thematic and non-linear, grouping images by resemblance rather than date. You start from the earliest images (Chauvet, Lascaux), move through Neolithic settlement and ritual objects (asking what an object does, not just what it depicts), and end by building your first board — the course in miniature. The same move returns at larger scale in A4 and the End-of-Term retrospective.

The darkroom session on day three shows how an image is something light did to a surface, not just a picture on a screen. This idea carries into Unit 4 in December.

Key theorist: Aby Warburg. Key material: cave imagery, Neolithic figures, ritual objects.
