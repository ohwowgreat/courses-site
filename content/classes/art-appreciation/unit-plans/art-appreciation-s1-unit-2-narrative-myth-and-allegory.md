# Art Appreciation S1 Unit 2: Narrative, Myth and Allegory

[[classes/art-appreciation/art-appreciation|Art Appreciation]] · [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]] · Unit 2 of 5 · [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-s1-unit-1-origins-and-ancestral-memory|← Unit 1]] | [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-s1-unit-3-food-the-table-and-ethics|Unit 3 →]]

## What this unit does

How images carry story without words. The first half builds vocabulary — narrative (where a still image puts time), myth (shared shorthand that needs an audience that already knows), allegory (the image that means elsewhere), cycle — and turns it into a made thing, the ten-frame storyboard carried on framing alone. The second half is the pair that carries the unit: Titian's *Venus of Urbino* ("what it asks you to accept as natural") against Manet's *Olympia* ("locate the exact refusals"), with Gentileschi's *Judith Slaying Holofernes* as the close-looking hinge on the problem of the witness. The critique flow (describe, account, judge) is taught cold at the top of lesson 04; the storyboard is explicit rehearsal for the A2 comparison — both are about reading what a picture decided.
