# Unit 2: Narrative, Myth and Allegory

[[classes/art-appreciation/art-appreciation|Art Appreciation]] · [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]] · Unit 2 of 5 · [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-s1-unit-1-origins-and-ancestral-memory|← Unit 1]] | [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-s1-unit-3-food-the-table-and-ethics|Unit 3 →]]

## What this unit does

This unit is about how images carry a story without words. The first half builds your vocabulary — narrative (where a still image places time), myth (shared shorthand that needs an audience who already knows the story), allegory (the image that means something elsewhere), and cycle — and turns it into a made thing: a ten-frame storyboard that has to work on framing alone.

The second half centres on a pair of paintings: Titian's *Venus of Urbino* (what it asks you to accept as natural) against Manet's *Olympia* (where its refusals are located), with Gentileschi's *Judith Slaying Holofernes* as a close-looking study of the problem of the witness. The critique flow (describe, account, judge) is taught from scratch at the top of Lesson 04. The storyboard is direct rehearsal for the A2 comparison — both are about reading what a picture decided.
