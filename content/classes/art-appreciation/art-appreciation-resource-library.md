# Art Appreciation Resource Library

## Readings — assigned

| Reading | Where it lands |
|---|---|
| **John Berger, "The Eaters and the Eaten"** | The anchor of the food strand. Its opening line — *"To eat is to appropriate, to take into ourselves the lives of others"* — heads the Food and Ethics unit. Serves U3 |
| **John Berger, *Ways of Seeing*, ch. 1** | [[classes/art-appreciation/lesson-plans/art-appreciation-s1-lesson-10-berger-and-the-reproduction\|L10 Berger and the reproduction]] |
| **Walter Benjamin, "The Work of Art in the Age of Its Technological Reproducibility" (2nd version, 1936/2008)** | [[classes/art-appreciation/lesson-plans/art-appreciation-s1-lesson-11-aura-and-the-poor-image\|L11 Aura and the poor image]] — the aura half |
| **Hito Steyerl, "In Defense of the Poor Image"** | L11 — the poor-image half. The two readings are read against each other |
| **Susan Sontag, "In Plato's Cave"** | [[classes/art-appreciation/lesson-plans/art-appreciation-s1-lesson-12-photography-and-after\|L12 Photography and after]]. This is *On Photography*'s first essay, extracted — the full book is on the reference shelf |
| **Dean Kissick, "The Painted Protest"** · **"Hello Darkness"** | Contemporary art-world criticism; fits [[classes/art-appreciation/lesson-plans/art-appreciation-s1-lesson-13-the-artworld-and-the-brief\|L13 The artworld and the brief]] |

## Further reading

| Reading | Note |
|---|---|
| **Michael Baxandall, *Painting and Experience in Fifteenth-Century Italy*** | The source of "painting is a deposit of a social relationship." Underpins the patronage strand |
| **Francis Haskell, *Patrons and Painters: Art and Society in Baroque Italy*** | Baroque patronage. A 25-page extract (pp. 120–145) is the teaching text; the full book is the shelf copy |
| **Sarah Thornton, *Seven Days in the Art World*** | Ethnography of the contemporary art world — the empirical counterpart to Danto's philosophy at L13 |
| **Susan Sontag, *On Photography*** (full) | The complete book; "In Plato's Cave" is its first essay |
| **John Berger, *Portraits*** | |
| **The Image Object Post-Internet** | Post-internet art theory; pairs with the Ulman and Horvitz videos |
| **Documents of Contemporary Art** — 16 volumes: Abstraction · Appropriation · Beauty · Documentary · Failure · Nature · Networks · Sound · Systems · The Archive · The Artist's Joke · The Cute · The Everyday · The Sublime · Time · Utopias | The Whitechapel/MIT anthology series. A **thematic shelf, not a syllabus** — each volume is an edited collection of short primary texts. Use it to source one or two excerpts when your Atlas Book theme needs theory |

The DOCA series is the most reusable resource here for the **Atlas Book capstone**: if you are working on masks and identity, or screens, or ruins, you can go to a volume rather than open search.
