# Art Appreciation

> **Start here:** [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]] · [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-semester-2-outline|Semester 2 Outline]] · [[classes/art-appreciation/assessments/art-appreciation-s1-assessments|S1 Assessments]] · [[classes/art-appreciation/art-appreciation-resource-library|Reading list]] · unit docs in `unit-plans/` · lesson docs in `lesson-plans/` · [[classes/art-appreciation/course-calendar|Calendar]]

An **elective, internally assessed** course at BNDS — no syllabus code, no external paper. AP-derived in ancestry, run under the BNDS A-Level Handbook rules ([[shared/bnds-assessment-framework]]); ⚠ which handbook governs is itself an open item. Cohort: **mixed G11 + G12, ~11 students**, an ESL room (critique protocol adapted: write before speaking).

**Method (verbatim from the schedule):** "Aby Warburg's Mnemosyne Atlas: thematic and non-linear, not chronological. Units are constellations, not periods. The throughline is that the structures of the art world are accumulated conventions rather than natural laws." The three constellation assessments (A1 board → A4 synthesis → End-of-Term retrospective) are the same move at increasing scale.

**Teaching pattern ✅ confirmed 2026-07-19:** **Mon P4 (45') · Tue P6+7 (DOUBLE, 90') · Wed P1 (45') · Fri P2 (45')** — four teaching days, five periods, one double, **no Thursday**. The S1 plan was **redrawn onto it the same day**: same teaching minutes (225/week), but 65 teaching days instead of 80, and the four Thursday-scheduled attainments moved to Tuesday doubles — which suits them, since each is an in-class essay or constellation build previously squeezed into 45 minutes.

## Semester 1 (fully planned)

Five units across Autumn 2026-27 — detail in the [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-semester-1-plan|Semester 1 Plan]]:

| Unit | Weeks | Theme |
|---|---|---|
| U1 | W1–3 | Origins and Ancestral Memory (caves, survival, ritual objects, the first board) |
| U2 | W4–7 | Narrative, Myth and Allegory (storyboards; Gentileschi; **Titian vs Manet**) |
| U3 | W8–12 | Food, the Table and Ethics (*Eat Drink Man Woman*; Heda; Last Supper; Qingming scroll; Cassatt/Rosler/Weems) |
| U4 | W13–17 | Images, Mediation and Modernity (Berger; ukiyo-e; Benjamin; Steyerl; Sontag; Greenberg; Danto) |
| U5 | W19 | Semester close: the constellation retrospective (W18 is the winter break) |

**Theorist spine in teaching order: Warburg → Berger → Benjamin → Steyerl → Sontag → Greenberg → Danto.**

Majors: A1 board (Fri 2026-09-18), A2 Titian-vs-Manet in-class essay (Tue 2026-10-13), A3 food/table/ethics essay (Tue 2026-11-17), A4 synthesis constellation (Tue 2026-12-15), End-of-Term retrospective (Tue 2027-01-05, 20%).

Semester 2 exists as a titled 16-week outline only (art market, patronage, *La Chimera*, Shanzhai, Atlas Book capstone) — [[classes/art-appreciation/unit-plans/art-appreciation-semester-2-outline|Semester 2 Outline]].
