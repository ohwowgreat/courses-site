## Goal

The board is the course in miniature and this is the first time they do it, so the failure mode is predictable: "they will make a mood board and call the adjacencies obvious." The justification is what separates the two, and it is the thing to be hard about. Days one and two are content, days three to five the build — and the build must not start early: "Students who begin arranging before they have enough candidates arrange what they have."

## How it runs

- **Day 1 · Mon 14 Sept · `U1·L10`** — Neolithic: settlement and the made object. What changes in images when people stop moving.
- **Day 2 · Tue 15 Sept · `U1·L11`** — ritual objects and ancestral presence: what an object *does*, not only what it depicts. G12 predicted grades due today — expect absences.
- **Day 3 · Wed 16 Sept · `U1·L12`** — constellation workshop: two unlike images, one defended pairing, out loud, everybody. Direct practice for A1.
- **Day 4 · Thu 17 Sept · `U1·L13`** — build the Unit 1 board on the wall; nothing fixed today.
- **Day 5 · Fri 18 Sept · `U1·L14`** — final hang (20 min), justification written (20 min). **A1 collected. Boards stay up.**

## Assessment

- **A1 sits Fri 2026-09-18** — Unit 1 board and justification. Criteria sheet issued with the announcement on Fri 2026-09-04, not on the day. Marked on: every adjacency justified with a reason that could not be moved to a different pair; argument legible from the layout before a word is read; objects described by what they do.
- The group board plus individual write-up split makes the grade half collective — "say early how that works or you will spend day five arguing about it instead of hanging."
