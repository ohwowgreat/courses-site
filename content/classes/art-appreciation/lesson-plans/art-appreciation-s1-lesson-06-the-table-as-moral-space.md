## Goal

Unit 3 opens on a film because **"the table is easier to see when it is moving."** Ang Lee's cooking scenes are the argument: everything the family cannot say is said through food, and the students will notice the noticing before they notice the structure. The screening runs Tuesday and Wednesday as one continuing session — with a real, unresolved runtime problem (see notes).

## How it runs

- **Day 1 · Mon 19 Oct · `U3·L1`** — the table as moral space, the frame for the whole unit; first look at three paintings of meals: who is where.
- **Days 2–3 · Tue 20 – Wed 21 Oct · `U3·L2-3`** — *Eat Drink Man Woman* screening as one two-day session: day one lands then screens 40 minutes; day two screens 45 with no landing, picking up on the cut.
- **Thu 22 Oct · `U3·L4`** — the film's table: one meal replayed twice, then the written reading — "an argument nobody can make aloud". The reading is the deliverable.
- **Fri 23 Oct · `U3·L5`** — seminar: food, class, and who is not in the picture. LB3 posted. Autumn Field Trip may take this day.

(The raw file's day labels slip after the two-day screening — "Day 5 of 5" / "Day 6 of 5" — but dates and session codes are unambiguous.)

## Assessment

- **LB3** — Fri 2026-10-23, seminar posting. Moves if the field trip takes Friday.
- **PROPOSED deliverable**: the one-page written reading of a single meal sequence (Thu), grounded in the `U3·L4` goal line.
