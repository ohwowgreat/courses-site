## Goal

"It reads as one lesson and it will not feel like one": a Monday–Tuesday stub, then the room empties for G11 Juniors Days, then a full week. **A3 must be announced on day two** — fourteen days before Thursday 19 November is Thursday 5 November, a Juniors Day, so Tuesday 3 November is the last session both halves of the class attend before the deadline. "If that Tuesday goes, A3 moves, not the announcement."

## How it runs

- **Day 1 · Mon 2 Nov · `U3·L11`** — Heda: the still life as inventory. Name every object and who owns it. Standalone.
- **Day 2 · Tue 3 Nov · `U3·L12`** — **A3 ANNOUNCED** (Unit 3 essay, Thu 19 Nov) in the first ten minutes. Then Heda again, the same painting, alone this time, further than yesterday. LB5 posted. Then three days out.
- **Day 3 · Mon 9 Nov · `U3·L13`** — Leonardo, *The Last Supper*: find the moment Leonardo chose, and say why that one and not the one before it.
- **Day 4 · Tue 10 Nov · `U3·L14`** — Bruegel and Steen: the disorderly table, taken seriously as a subject rather than comedy.
- **Day 5 · Wed 11 Nov · `U3·L15`** — the Qingming scroll, unrolled, followed by food along its length — 35 of the 45 minutes.
- **Day 6 · Thu 12 Nov · `U3·L16`** — the moral image of work: when did work become worth painting, and for whom.
- **Day 7 · Fri 13 Nov · `U3·L17`** — write the Heda paragraph; swap and cut one adjective from someone else's. CS4 collected. Direct practice for A3.

## Assessment

- Tue 2026-11-03 — the last both-cohort session before the deadline; 16 days' notice.
- **LB5** — Tue 2026-11-03, posted from the second Heda session.
- **CS4** — Fri 2026-11-13, the Heda paragraph: "one paragraph that earns every adjective in it."
