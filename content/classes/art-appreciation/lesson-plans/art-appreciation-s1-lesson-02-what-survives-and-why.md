## Goal

The notice week and the cave week at the same time — and the notice is the part that cannot slip. Fourteen days before Friday 18 September is Friday 4 September exactly, with no session before it, so if A1 is not announced on day one of this lesson "it cannot be set at all this term". Put it first, in the first ten minutes, before the content. Then five days on people who left no writing. The temptation is to fill the silence with anthropology: resist it. **"The wall is the evidence"** and they have to argue from the wall.

## How it runs

- **Day 1 · Fri 4 Sept · `U1·L4`** — **A1 ANNOUNCED** in the first ten minutes: the board, the justification, due Fri 18 Sept. Then: what survives, and why — name what had to happen for an object to reach you at all.
- **Day 2 · Mon 7 Sept · `U1·L5`** — Lascaux and Chauvet, first pass, no commentary; describe round. Chauvet reading set.
- **Day 3 · Tue 8 Sept · `U1·L6`** — hands, animals and the dark: account for the choice of animal, wall, and depth.
- **Day 4 · Wed 9 Sept · `U1·L7`** — why make a mark: one reason argued, evidenced from the wall.
- **Day 5 · Thu 10 Sept · `U1·L8`** — read a cave wall the way Warburg read a panel, by proximity. Two paired images; the written panel reading is the deliverable.
- **Day 6 · Fri 11 Sept · `U1·L9`** — seminar: what the first images wanted. Thirty minutes; LB1 posted from what happens inside it.

## Assessment

- Fri 2026-09-04 — the 14-day rule met exactly; the most fragile date in the semester.
- **LB1** — Fri 2026-09-11, posted from the seminar. Graded on whether they took a position and moved it — a behavior, so say that is what is being watched, "otherwise the confident ones perform and the quiet ones lose."
- **PROPOSED deliverable**: the one-page panel reading (day 5) — direct rehearsal for the A1 justification, the same move at greater length.
