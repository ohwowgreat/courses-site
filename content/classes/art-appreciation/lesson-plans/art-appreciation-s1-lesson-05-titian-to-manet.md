## Goal

Opens on a debrief and closes on feedback, with the essay on day seven. The room has been away twelve days and the first thing back is their own storyboards returned — "the right way round: they see their own decisions before they look at anyone else's." The comparison is the whole point and it is where students reach for a list: "A list is not a comparison." The essay wants one claim about two pictures, and differences only count if they carry meaning.

## How it runs

- **Day 1 · Thu 8 Oct · `U2·L9`** — break-homework debrief: storyboards returned, ten minutes silent with the criteria, find the frame where the sequence stopped being legible. CS3 posted; assessment review protocol applies.
- **Day 2 · Fri 9 Oct · `U2·L10`** — Gentileschi's Judith, close looking: "describe the violence in the painting before you describe the painter."
- **Day 3 · Sat 10 Oct · `U2·L11`** — Judith and the problem of the witness: who does the picture put you next to? Make-up Saturday, Sports Day after lunch — expect a short room.
- **Day 4 · Mon 12 Oct · `U2·L12`** — Titian, *Venus of Urbino*: "what does it ask you to accept as natural." Reception reading set.
- **Day 5 · Tue 13 Oct · `U2·L13`** — Manet, *Olympia*, silent, no context: "locate the exact refusals Manet is making of Titian", one by one.
- **Day 6 · Wed 14 Oct · `U2·L14`** — comparison workshop: build the claim, test it against the pictures.
- **Day 7 · Thu 15 Oct · `U2·L15`** — **A2 sits.** In class, closed, 40 minutes. Oxbridge deadline today — check the G12 register.
- **Day 8 · Fri 16 Oct · `U2·L16`** — first read-back and the bridge into food. Foreign Culture Day risk: "if this falls, nothing is lost that the essay needed."
