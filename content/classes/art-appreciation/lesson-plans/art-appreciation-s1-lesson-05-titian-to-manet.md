## Goal

This lesson opens with a debrief and closes with feedback, with the essay in between. Your returned storyboards come back first, so you see your own decisions before looking at anyone else's. The essay is a comparison, not a list: a list is not a comparison. Make one claim about two pictures, and count differences only where they carry meaning.

## How it runs

- **Day 1 · Thu 8 Oct · `U2·L9`** — break-homework debrief: storyboards returned, ten minutes silent with the criteria, find the frame where the sequence stopped being legible. CS3 posted.
- **Day 2 · Fri 9 Oct · `U2·L10`** — Gentileschi's Judith, close looking: describe the violence in the painting before you describe the painter.
- **Day 3 · Sat 10 Oct · `U2·L11`** — Judith and the problem of the witness: who does the picture put you next to? Make-up Saturday, Sports Day after lunch.
- **Day 4 · Mon 12 Oct · `U2·L12`** — Titian, *Venus of Urbino*: what does it ask you to accept as natural. Reception reading set.
- **Day 5 · Tue 13 Oct · `U2·L13`** — Manet, *Olympia*, silent, no context: locate the exact refusals Manet is making of Titian, one by one.
