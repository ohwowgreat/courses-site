## Goal

G12 is at Seniors' Day all week, so nothing may carry from one day to the next and nothing assessable can sit here. "This is the one lesson in the semester that breaks the rule it is built on": five sessions that do not accumulate, because half the people who would accumulate them are not in the building. The through-line is thematic, not sequential — five different ways into the same question, any of which works alone. **"A G12 student who misses all five has missed content, not an argument."**

## How it runs

- **Day 1 · Mon 26 Oct · `U3·L6`** — shared-lunch prep: the meal as artwork, planned in pairs, decisions defended rather than preferred.
- **Day 2 · Tue 27 Oct · `U3·L7`** — still life and the ethics of abundance: what did this cost, who paid.
- **Day 3 · Wed 28 Oct · `U3·L8`** — individual reflection: your own table, "without turning it into a memory piece". The deliverable, finished today.
- **Day 4 · Thu 29 Oct · `U3·L9`** — what a shared meal makes visible that talking does not.
- **Day 5 · Fri 30 Oct · `U3·L10`** — seminar: is hospitality generosity or obligation. LB4 posted, for whoever is present.

## Assessment

- **The your-table reflection** — written and finished Wed 2026-10-28.
- **LB4** — Fri 2026-10-30, posted for the students in the room. "G12 is not penalised for being at Seniors' Day, and say so before anyone asks, because they will."
- Mid-term grades are due Thu 2026-10-29 and rest on A2 plus participation — "the marking has to be done before this week starts, not during it."
