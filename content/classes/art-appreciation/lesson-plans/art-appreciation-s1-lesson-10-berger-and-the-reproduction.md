## Goal

Seven teaching days across two weeks with the class split in both of them, so the real content is front-loaded into days one to three and the rest is self-contained by necessity. **A4 must be announced on day seven, the Tuesday** — fourteen days before Thursday 17 December is Thursday 3 December, a Juniors Day, so Tuesday 1 December is the last session both halves attend. G12 also carries a reduced load before the 1 December UC deadline, "which is why nothing is assessed in this lesson beyond two postings."

## How it runs

- **Day 1 · Mon 23 Nov · `U4·L1`** — Berger's argument stated flat: a reproduction is not a neutral copy. The first objection, taken seriously. Reading set.
- **Day 2 · Tue 24 Nov · `U4·L2`** — one painting, four contexts: change the words around an image and watch its meaning move.
- **Day 3 · Wed 25 Nov · `U4·L3`** — seminar: what did the painting lose, what did it gain, by becoming reproducible. LB6 posted.
- **Day 4 · Thu 26 Nov · `U4·L4`** — self-contained (G12 out): a reproduction read as an object with decisions of its own.
- **Day 5 · Fri 27 Nov · `U4·L5`** — self-contained (G12 out): write the caption that changes the picture, then write the honest one. The deliverable.
- **Day 6 · Mon 30 Nov · `U4·L6`** — Godard, *Goodbye to Language*, one extract: "sit with an image that refuses to add up, and stay with it." Standalone.
- **Day 7 · Tue 1 Dec · `U4·L7`** — **A4 ANNOUNCED** (Unit 4 synthesis, Thu 17 Dec), then the floating world as a market, not a mood. LB7 posted. The lesson file says "UC deadline today," but the [[calendar|school calendar]] puts the UC deadline on **Mon 2026-11-30** — the calendar wins on dates; either way G12 will be thin at the start of this week.

- **LB7** — Tue 2026-12-01, floating-world posting.
- Tue 2026-12-01 — last both-cohort session before the deadline; 16 days.
- The caption exercise is the ungraded deliverable both halves complete.
